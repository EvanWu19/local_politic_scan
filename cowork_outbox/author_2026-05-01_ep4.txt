ALEX: This week — April twenty-ninth — Maryland Matters reported that Maryland enacted three new immigration-related laws while a fourth, the Community Trust Act, sits on the Governor's desk. We covered the Trust Act at length in episode three, so just a recap there — that bill would limit when local police can hold someone solely on a federal immigration detainer, and the Governor's signing window closes the first week of May. The three bills that already became law are the more granular pieces.
JORDAN: What did the three already do.
ALEX: Bill one — protections for immigrant crime victims and witnesses, which clarifies that fear of immigration consequences cannot be used to coerce silence in a state-court proceeding. Bill two — a state-level requirement that local jails provide at least forty-eight hours notice to family members and counsel before transferring a detainee to ICE custody. Bill three — restrictions on the kinds of state databases that can be shared with federal immigration authorities without a judicial warrant.
JORDAN: All three of these passed before the session ended on April seventh.
ALEX: All three. They were signed into law in the package the Governor signed on April twenty-eighth — a batch that included over two hundred bills, some big, most small. Maryland Matters covered the package and flagged immigration specifically.

ALEX: Greenbelt is in Prince George's County, not Montgomery, so this is not your local police.
