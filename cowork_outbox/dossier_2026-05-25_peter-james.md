## 1. Resolving the person

This is the Peter James of Gaithersburg, Maryland, born ~1956 in Nashville, Tennessee, who is CEO of Crystal Clear Automation, LLC. He filed for Montgomery County Executive in the Democratic primary on February 24, 2026 — the same office he sought in 2022. He is the same person who ran as the Republican nominee for the U.S. House in Maryland's 4th Congressional District in the 2008 special and general elections, lost the 2012 Republican primary for Maryland's 6th Congressional District to incumbent Roscoe Bartlett, and was disqualified from the 2020 general election ballot in MD-6 after filing as an Independent. The lineage is documented in his Ballotpedia page and corroborated by Maryland State Board of Elections returns. [src: https://ballotpedia.org/Peter_James ; https://elections.maryland.gov/elections/2008/results/primary/congressional_district_04.html]

He is not to be confused with the British literary critic Peter James, the Canadian musician of the same name, or several other public figures with the name; the disambiguator is consistent residence in Gaithersburg/Germantown since at least 2008 and continuous ownership of Crystal Clear Automation since the LLC's 2015 formation in Maryland. [src: https://opencorporates.com/companies/us_md/W19786813 ; https://www.bizapedia.com/md/crystal-clear-automation-llc.html]

**Best-estimate birth year:** ~1956. The 2022 Bethesda Magazine candidacy story listed his age as 66 at filing in March 2022. That puts him at age 70 as of the June 23, 2026 primary. An exact birth date has not been surfaced in this run and is flagged in Section 7. [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2022/03/28/gaithersburg-man-with-tech-background-enters-county-executive-race/]

**Look-back window for Section 3 below:** age 70 − 20 = 50 years, so the window runs from roughly 1976 to present. In practice the public record is thin before his 2008 congressional run; pre-2008 facts are derived from autobiographical material on the candidate's sites and the Ballotpedia and Montgomery for All questionnaires.

## 3. Deep news history (1976–2026 window)

The public record before 2008 is sparse. The candidate has not been quoted in surfaced press coverage prior to his first congressional run. The deep-history items below are the substantive press and public-record events from the candidate-age window:

- **2008 — first congressional run, MD-4 (Republican).** James won a four-way Republican primary for the open MD-4 seat after eight-term Democrat Albert Wynn resigned mid-term. He then lost the June 17, 2008 special general election to Democrat Donna Edwards by roughly 81% – 17% (a Libertarian, Thibeaux Lincecum, was also on the ballot). He ran again in the November general for the full term and lost to Edwards by approximately 85% – 13%. Maryland State Board of Elections returns and Wikipedia's entry on the 2008 MD-4 special election both confirm these margins. [src: https://elections.maryland.gov/elections/2008/2008_special_4th_congressional_general_results.html ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maryland's_4th_congressional_district_special_election,_2008]
- **2012 — second congressional run, MD-6 (Republican).** Filed for the Republican primary in the redrawn MD-6 (Montgomery / Frederick / Western Maryland) against eight-term incumbent Roscoe Bartlett. Lost the primary; did not advance to the general election Bartlett went on to lose to Democrat John Delaney. [src: https://ballotpedia.org/Peter_James]
- **2020 — third congressional run, MD-6 (Independent).** Filed to appear on the November 2020 general-election ballot as an Independent. Ballotpedia records that he was disqualified from the general election; the specific basis for the disqualification (likely an unmet signature or filing-deadline requirement under Maryland Election Law §5-703 for unaffiliated candidates) has not been surfaced in this run and is flagged in Section 7. [src: https://ballotpedia.org/Peter_James]
- **March 2022 — first Montgomery County Executive run (Democrat).** Filed at the deadline, creating a five-way Democratic primary against incumbent Marc Elrich, Councilmember Hans Riemer, businessman David Blair, and Councilmember Tom Hucker. Bethesda Magazine's day-of story noted he had "always been more of an independent voter" but had registered as a Democrat to "reach the most people for the upcoming election." His core 2022 pitch was that Elrich, Riemer, Blair, and Hucker "do not have the technical skills to lead a county of over 1 million people, especially at a time when technology is evolving rapidly." [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2022/03/28/gaithersburg-man-with-tech-background-enters-county-executive-race/]
- **June – July 2022 — Personal Rapid Transit platform.** In the 2022 primary cycle he ran on, and was identified with, a proposal to build pod-based "personal rapid transit" along I-270 and major corridors instead of expanding bus rapid transit. The 2022 platform is the documentary ancestor of his 2026 autonomous-guideway proposal (see Section 4). [src: https://www.mocoalliance.org/uploads/4/8/8/6/48867647/peter-james-county-executive.pdf — MoCo Alliance candidate questionnaire, 2022]
- **July 19, 2022 — finished last in the Democratic primary.** Marc Elrich won renomination over David Blair by a narrow margin; James finished well off the top of the field. No precinct-level breakdown is summarized here.
- **February 24, 2026 — second Montgomery County Executive filing.** Filed at the close of the 2026 filing window, becoming the fifth Democrat in the primary alongside Councilmembers Friedson, Glass, and Jawando and challenger Mithun Banerjee. [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/02/25/additional-county-executive-candidates-emerge-ahead-of-tuesday-deadline/]
- **April 16, 2026 — countywide candidate forum.** Participated in the multi-candidate forum reported by Maryland Matters and WTOP. Forum coverage characterized him as the most ideologically heterodox candidate on stage — emphasizing technological disruption and a "we're in the most difficult times" framing. [src: https://marylandmatters.org/2026/04/16/democratic-candidates-for-montgomery-countys-top-job-make-pitches-at-forum/ ; https://wtop.com/montgomery-county/2026/04/democratic-candidates-for-montgomery-countys-top-job-make-pitches-at-forum/]
- **April 2026 — Montgomery for All 2026 candidate questionnaire.** Responded with a detailed policy platform now used as the most coherent contemporary statement of his positions. The questionnaire is the source for most of the Section 4 policy lines below. [src: https://www.montgomery4all.org/peter-james-2026]

Highlighted his small-business background, the AI/robotics company, and his "stop ICE abuse" framing for immigration. [src: https://wjla.com/news/local/peter-james-montgomery-county-maryland-executive-candidate-democrat-primary-election-race-campaign-political]

## 4. Political track record and platform

He has never held elected office. The "track record" below is therefore a record of stated positions across his five candidacies (2008, 2012, 2020, 2022, 2026), with attention to where positions have shifted and where they have stayed constant.

### 4A. Positions across cycles (continuity vs. shift)

- **Party affiliation.** Republican in 2008 and 2012; Independent in 2020; Democrat in 2022 and 2026. He has publicly framed this as a tactical choice to reach the most voters in a heavily Democratic county, not a conversion. [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2022/03/28/gaithersburg-man-with-tech-background-enters-county-executive-race/]
- **Technology-led governance.** Constant across all cycles. The candidate has consistently positioned himself as the technology executive who would bring engineering rigor to government.
- **Transit.** Constant across 2022 and 2026: the county should pursue automated guideway transit ("personal rapid transit" in 2022; autonomous self-driving vehicles on elevated guideways in 2026) instead of standard bus rapid transit. The 2026 version puts hard cost numbers on the table — elevated guideway at roughly one-quarter the cost of dedicated-lane BRT, at-grade guideway at $500,000 per mile, and roughly half the cost of light rail. [src: https://www.montgomery4all.org/peter-james-2026 ; https://bethesdamagazine.com/2022/03/28/gaithersburg-man-with-tech-background-enters-county-executive-race/]

### 4B. 2026 platform (as of the April 2026 Montgomery for All questionnaire and forum statements)

**Taxes and the county budget.** Opposes the property-tax increase under discussion in the FY27 budget cycle. Frames the position from a small-business-owner perspective. [src: https://www.thebanner.com/voter-guide/2026/montgomery-county/montgomery-county-executive-52K4MQ564ZDHTKO6TYERB44GHY/]

**Immigration and ICE.** Pledges to use the county's contracting and investment power to divest from "bad actors" — companies that contract with U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement. The campaign domain tagline is "Stop ICE abuse and the rise of Corporate ..." [src: https://peter4moco.com/] He has not, in surfaced materials, taken a position for or against the existing county Trust Act / Values Act framework. Flagged in Section 7.

**Transit.** Replace the county's planned Bus Rapid Transit corridors with autonomous-vehicle elevated guideway transit. Cost claim: roughly one-quarter the cost of dedicated-lane BRT; at-grade guideway $500,000 per mile; about half the cost of light rail. [src: https://www.montgomery4all.org/peter-james-2026]

**Housing.** Use robotics in the construction of affordable housing, ostensibly to drive per-unit costs down. Supports "lower densities and more housing types in all zones" rather than "packing low income households into smaller spaces." This is in explicit contrast to the More Housing N.O.W. zoning text amendment (ZTA 25-02) passed by the council July 22, 2025 over a 8–3 vote. [src: https://www.montgomery4all.org/peter-james-2026]

**Education and MCPS.** Would "not fund the county school system in its current form" as County Executive — and would either fold MCPS functions into county government or change the board's governance structure. The exact mechanic he proposes ("require its board be elected just like the school board") is paraphrased from the Banner voter guide and is not perfectly clear; the most charitable reading is that he favors a more direct executive-style accountability for MCPS than the current elected-board / superintendent model. [src: https://www.thebanner.com/voter-guide/2026/montgomery-county/montgomery-county-executive-52K4MQ564ZDHTKO6TYERB44GHY/]

**Economy and AI.** Positions himself as the candidate most attuned to economic disruption from artificial intelligence. Has warned about "the most difficult times" ahead and urges voters to consider "nontraditional solutions." [src: https://marylandmatters.org/2026/04/16/democratic-candidates-for-montgomery-countys-top-job-make-pitches-at-forum/]

## 5. Controversies and notable press

Neutral framing:

- **No criminal charges, indictments, or convictions** have been surfaced.
- **No FEC complaints, ethics complaints, or civil lawsuits** naming him personally have been surfaced. His three federal congressional campaigns presumably filed FEC reports on the standard quarterly schedule, but the public record contains no FEC enforcement action surfaced for this run.
- **2020 disqualification, MD-6 Independent run.** The fact that he was disqualified is documented; the basis is not. Under Maryland Election Law, an unaffiliated congressional candidate must collect petition signatures equal to 1% of the registered voters in the district by the August deadline preceding the November general election. The most common reason for an unaffiliated congressional candidate to be removed from the ballot in Maryland is signature shortfall. This is the inference; the specific stipulation in his case is not in the public record surfaced for this run.
- **Pattern of low-vote-share campaigns.** Across 2008, 2012, 2020, and 2022, he has not crossed the threshold of competitive viability in any single race. The 2008 general-election result against Donna Edwards (13% Republican share in a D+25 district) is consistent with the partisan baseline rather than evidence of personal weakness; the 2022 Democratic-primary finish is more genuinely informative. The 2026 race is being characterized in mainstream press as a four-way contest among Friedson, Glass, Jawando, and Banerjee, with James positioned outside the contested tier. [src: https://www.thebanner.com/voter-guide/2026/montgomery-county/montgomery-county-executive-52K4MQ564ZDHTKO6TYERB44GHY/]
- **Self-positioning as outsider with engineering expertise.** This is a recurring framing across cycles — not a controversy per se, but a posture the listener should weigh against the candidate's actual delivery record (none in elected office; in the private sector, a small AI-robotics R&D firm whose customer list, revenue, and headcount are not public).

## 6. Stance on listener-flagged topics

### 6A. Sanctuary policy / undocumented immigration (listener priority #1)

The listener has been asking, week after week, which Democratic primary candidate **opposes** the existing sanctuary framework. Peter James does **not** oppose it. His stated position points in the other direction: divestment from companies contracting with ICE. The campaign homepage tagline frames his position as "Stop ICE abuse." He has not, in surfaced materials, taken a position on the December 2025 Trust Act (Bill 32-25) or the March 2026 County Values Act (Bill 3-26); given the divestment posture, the most plausible reading is that he would have supported both. [src: https://peter4moco.com/ ; https://wjla.com/news/local/peter-james-montgomery-county-maryland-executive-candidate-democrat-primary-election-race-campaign-political]

**Bottom-line assessment:** supports the existing sanctuary framework, with an additional layer (ICE-contractor divestment).

### 6B. Andrew Friedson's fiscal record (listener priority #2)

Not directly relevant to a Peter James dossier, but worth noting that James is in the same anti-tax-increase camp as Friedson on the FY27 budget question — both have opposed the proposed property-tax increase publicly. The difference is that Friedson has cast actual votes against Elrich tax increases in 2020, 2023, 2025 and 2026 from a council seat; James has cast no votes.

**Bottom-line assessment on James:** opposes the proposed property tax increase, on a small-business-owner rationale.

### 6C. Wootton High School closure / Crown relocation (listener priority #3)

No surfaced statement on the Wootton-Crown relocation specifically. The closest position is his broader proposal to restructure MCPS governance and rethink county funding of the school system. The listener should treat his MCPS position as "system overhaul" rather than "Wootton specifically" — the latter is what Friedson testified on October 28, 2025 and what is moving through the MCPS Board of Education timeline.

**Bottom-line assessment:** unclear on the specific Wootton question; the broader MCPS-restructuring posture would, if implemented, change who controls the Wootton decision.

### 6D. Ben Kramer's career-long voting record (listener priority #4)

Not applicable to a Peter James dossier (Kramer is the state senator candidate, district 19).
