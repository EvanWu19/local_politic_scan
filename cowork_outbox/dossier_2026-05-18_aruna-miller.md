## 2. Biographical foundation

**Demographics.** Born 1964 (age 62 in 2026). Indian-American; she is the first immigrant and first Asian American to hold statewide office in Maryland. Practicing Hindu. Speaks English and Telugu.

**Socioeconomic.** Career civil engineer in transportation planning before entering politics. Family financial disclosures filed annually with the Maryland State Ethics Commission. Net worth not publicly itemized at a granular level but disclosures place her in the high-net-worth professional bracket typical of senior state executives. Homeowner in Darnestown.

**Education.** Public school in New York; civil engineering degree from Missouri S&T (then Missouri-Rolla), 1989.

**Career.** Twenty-five years as a transportation engineer for the Montgomery County Department of Transportation. Specialized in roadway design and traffic engineering. Left the county DOT in 2010 to run for state office.

## 3. Political record

**Maryland House of Delegates, District 15 (2011–2019).** Elected in 2010, re-elected 2014. Represented western Montgomery County. Notable legislative work:
- Lead sponsor on multiple transportation funding bills, leveraging her engineering background.
- Vice-chair of the Appropriations Committee's Transportation and Environment Subcommittee.
- Co-sponsor on the 2017 Maryland Trust Act (early version of state-level limits on cooperation with federal immigration enforcement).
- Voted yes on the 2018 paid sick leave override of then-Governor Hogan's veto.

**2018 congressional run, MD-6.** Ran for the open Democratic nomination after Rep. John Delaney left the seat to run for president. Finished second in a crowded primary, losing to David Trone. Raised over $2 million; the race was noted for being one of the most expensive Maryland primary races to that point. Did not return to state office that cycle.

**Executive director, Indian American Impact Fund (2019–2021).** National political organizing nonprofit focused on Indian-American electoral participation.

**Lieutenant Governor, 2023–present.** Selected as running mate by Wes Moore in late 2021; the ticket won the 2022 Democratic primary and the November general election. Sworn in January 2023.

## 4. Lt. Governor portfolio

Maryland Lt. Governors have no statutory portfolio of their own; the role is what the Governor delegates. Under Moore, Miller has been assigned three substantive portfolios:

**Service Year Option program.** Maryland's first-in-the-nation paid service year for high school graduates. Miller has been the public face of the rollout. The program enrolled approximately 200 fellows in the first cohort (2023–2024) and was expanded to roughly 400 in the second cohort. Federal AmeriCorps cuts in 2025–2026 have complicated the program's funding model; Miller has been the administration's point person on lobbying CMS and Congress on the AmeriCorps question.

**Economic competitiveness.** Co-chair of the Governor's Economic Competitiveness Cabinet. Focus on attracting biotech and quantum-computing employers to the I-270 corridor — which runs through the listener's district.

**Power and Purpose.** Public-private leadership development program launched April 2026 in Silver Spring (covered in today's episode three). Not a state agency; convened by the LG's office in partnership with the Maryland Philanthropy Network.
