# Andrew Friedson — Candidate Dossier

**Office sought:** Montgomery County Executive (Democratic primary, June 23, 2026)
**Current office:** Montgomery County Council, District 1 (Bethesda, Chevy Chase, Potomac, North Bethesda) — second term; Council President 2023–2024; chair of the Planning, Housing, and Parks (PHP) Committee
**Party:** Democrat
**Dossier compiled:** 2026-04-30 from public sources (web searches; see citations below)

> Caveat for listener Runyu: this dossier was assembled from public press, council press releases, and the candidate's official site. I have **not** been able to pull individual roll-call vote records line-by-line from the County Council's legislative database in this run; bill numbers and outcomes below are sourced from the press releases that announced each vote. Where a vote count is not in the source, I say so.

---

## 1. Bio

Andrew Friedson is a Montgomery County native, raised in the county's public school system, and has spent his career inside Maryland fiscal policy: he served as senior advisor to former state Comptroller Peter Franchot before running for the Council District 1 seat in 2018. He was first elected to the County Council in 2018, re-elected in 2022, and served as Council President in 2023–2024. He chairs the Council's Planning, Housing, and Parks Committee. He launched his campaign for County Executive on June 23, 2025, and on April 23, 2026 received in-Council endorsements from District 2's Marilyn Balcombe and District 7's Dawn Luedtke. He is married to Isabel Friedson and lives in Bethesda. ([andrewfriedson.com — About](https://andrewfriedson.com/meet-andrew/), [Maryland Matters — campaign launch](https://marylandmatters.org/2025/06/23/friedson-kicks-off-campaign-for-montgomery-county-executive/), [Bethesda Magazine — Balcombe / Luedtke endorsement](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/04/23/balcombe-luedtke-endorse-friedson-county-executive/))

## 2. Voting record by topic

The Council's legislative database (mococouncil.org / montgomerycountymd.gov press releases) is the authoritative source for individual bill votes. Where I have a verifiable bill number and outcome, it's listed. Where I only have a press release that confirms Friedson's position but does not name the roll-call count, that's noted.

**Property tax and county budget**
- Friedson has voted **against** county property-tax increases proposed by County Executive Marc Elrich in 2020, 2023, 2025, and 2026, and has positioned himself publicly as the Council's most consistent fiscal-discipline voice. ([andrewfriedson.com — About](https://andrewfriedson.com/meet-andrew/))
- Authored legislation expanding the **county supplement to the Homeowners' Tax Credit**, a means-tested property-tax credit. (Bill number not surfaced in this run; flagged as an open question.) ([andrewfriedson.com — About](https://andrewfriedson.com/meet-andrew/))
- Voted for the **FY26 Operating Budget** ($7B+, including ~$3.6B for MCPS = 99.8% of the Board of Education's request) **without** a property-tax increase. The preliminary agreement was unanimous among the 11 councilmembers. ([Montgomery Perspective — FY26 budget details](https://montgomeryperspective.com/2025/05/15/council-releases-details-of-fy26-operating-budget/))
- Voted for the **FY25 Operating Budget** ($7.1B; 99.2% of MCPS request) without a tax increase, while preserving reserves above 10.8%. ([Press release — FY25](https://www2.montgomerycountymd.gov/mcgportalapps/Press_Detail.aspx?Item_ID=45249))

**Education / MCPS funding**
- Friedson supports robust school funding but has explicitly opposed raising the property tax to do it; he and County Executive Elrich have feuded publicly over this trade-off. ([Bethesda Magazine — Elrich / Friedson MCPS funding fight](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2024/05/22/elrich-friedson-butt-heads-over-mcps-funding/))
- April 28, 2026: sent a letter to MCPS and county police asking them to "develop a strategy" to permanently station police officers in every county high school — sent **two days before** the April 30 Blake High shooting. The Community Engagement Officer (CEO) program currently in place replaced the older School Resource Officer (SRO) program that had been scrapped after equity concerns. Friedson's letter has revived that debate. ([Bethesda Magazine — Friedson police letter](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/04/30/mcps-police-officers-in-schools/), [WTOP — police in MoCo high schools](https://wtop.com/montgomery-county/2026/04/theres-a-push-to-put-police-in-every-high-school-permanently-in-montgomery-county/))

**Wootton High School relocation (listener-flagged)**
- October 28, 2025 testimony to the MCPS Board of Education: Friedson **explicitly called for full renovation of the existing Wootton building** rather than relocation, and credits Rockville Mayor Monique Ashton and Rockville Councilmember Adam Van Grack with shaping that testimony. Quote: "The idea of relocating Wootton to Crown never even occurred to me until MCPS proposed it… I meant what I said then, and I stand by it now."
- After the MCPS Board approved Option H (relocation to the new Crown High School in Gaithersburg, taking effect 2027–2028), Friedson publicly criticized the **process** — citing a lack of transparency. ([Montgomery Perspective — Wootton election issue](https://montgomeryperspective.com/2026/02/10/wootton-high-school-is-becoming-a-major-election-issue/), [Bethesda Magazine — MCPS chief recommends relocation](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/02/05/wootton-crown-relocation/))
- The county executive does **not** vote on individual MCPS school-closure decisions — those belong to the Board of Education — but the executive shapes the capital budget that funds school renovation, which is the lever Friedson would have if he wins.

**Housing / land use**
- **Lead sponsor**, ZTA 25-02 ("More Housing N.O.W."): allows duplex/triplex/townhome/apartment housing along county transit corridors (streets ≥3 lanes / >100 ft) and requires 15% workforce-affordable housing in qualifying projects. The bill **passed 8–3** on July 22, 2025 after a tense Council session. Co-lead: At-Large Councilmember Natali Fani-González. Critics on the Council — most prominently Will Jawando — say it favors developer interests; supporters call it the most consequential pro-housing zoning change in a decade. ([Bethesda Magazine — workforce housing 8-3 vote](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2025/07/22/workforce-housing-zoning-passage/), [Bethesda Magazine — More Housing N.O.W. bills](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2025/02/04/agenda-insights-more-housing-now/))
- **Lead sponsor**, Bill 2-25 (affordable housing; details not fully sourced — flagged as open question). Cosponsored by Luedtke, Balcombe, Sayles, and Council President Stewart.
- Sponsored the **FAITH ZTA**, a zoning text amendment making it easier for houses of worship to build affordable housing on their land. ([andrewfriedson.com — About](https://andrewfriedson.com/meet-andrew/))
- Critic of the county's existing rent-control policy; argues it discourages new construction. ([Bethesda Magazine — rent control comments](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2025/06/06/friedson-rent-control-moco/))

**Climate**
- **Co-sponsor**, Bill 2-26 (Taxation & Fuel-Energy Tax Amendments) with Councilmembers Luedtke and Glass — passed March 18, 2026; expands Green Bank funding for climate-resiliency projects. ([Montgomery County Green Bank press release](https://mcgreenbank.org/council-passes-climate-resiliency-legislation-sponsored-by-councilmembers-luedtke-friedson-and-glass/))

**Immigration / sanctuary policy (listener-flagged)**
- **Co-sponsor**, Bill 32-25 — the "Trust Act" (introduced December 2025, passed unanimously). Reaffirms that all residents — regardless of immigration status — can access county services without fear or discrimination. Friedson on the floor: *"We're standing up against the terror that ICE has sown in communities across the country because no family should have to live in fear."*
- **Co-sponsor**, Bill 3-26 — the "County Values Act" (passed unanimously March 24, 2026). Lead sponsor: Kristin Mink. Mandates clear judicial-warrant signage and staff training in county-controlled buildings, and **prohibits immigration-enforcement activities in county-owned parking lots, garages, and vacant lots**. Friedson is one of eight cosponsors. ([Press release — County Values Act](https://www2.montgomerycountymd.gov/mcgportalapps/Press_Detail.aspx?Item_ID=48442&Dept=1), [Press release — Trust Act intro](https://www2.montgomerycountymd.gov/mcgportalapps/Press_Detail.aspx?Item_ID=47996))

**Public safety / quality of life**
- Lead co-sponsor (with Luedtke), Bill 13-25 (unlicensed commercial house parties): tightens licensing, raises penalties on for-profit unlicensed residential events. ([Press release](https://www2.montgomerycountymd.gov/mcgportalapps/Press_Detail.aspx?Item_ID=47373&Dept=1))

## 3. Public statements & policy positions

- **On taxes and affordability:** "We have to figure out a way to manage government" — that raising taxes "is completely unsustainable." ([Bethesda Magazine — candidate forum](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/04/16/seven-candidates-one-stage-county-executive-hopefuls-make-their-pitch/))
- **On schools:** Supports funding MCPS but opposes property-tax hikes to do it; on FY26 he voted yes to a budget that funded 99.8% of the Board's request without a tax increase. ([Bethesda Magazine — Elrich/Friedson](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2024/05/22/elrich-friedson-butt-heads-over-mcps-funding/))
- **On Wootton:** Position is on record from October 2025 testimony — full renovation, not relocation. ([Montgomery Perspective](https://montgomeryperspective.com/2026/02/10/wootton-high-school-is-becoming-a-major-election-issue/))
- **On immigration:** Co-sponsored both the Trust Act and the County Values Act. By any reasonable reading, he is **not** "against sanctuary-style protections" — he voted for the strongest pieces the county has passed. (Same press releases above.)
- **On police in schools:** Wants permanent officers in every high school; argues "a permanently assigned officer on-site allows for relationship-building with students as a community policing best practice." ([Bethesda Magazine](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/04/30/mcps-police-officers-in-schools/))
- **On rent control:** Argues it stifles new development. ([Bethesda Magazine](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2025/06/06/friedson-rent-control-moco/))

## 5. Controversies and notable press

- **More Housing N.O.W. backlash.** The 8–3 council vote on July 22, 2025 was preceded by months of community organizing against the bill in single-family neighborhoods. Friedson and Jawando feuded publicly during PHP Committee work sessions. Jawando has accused Friedson of being too close to developer interests. ([Bethesda Magazine — workforce housing](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2025/07/22/workforce-housing-zoning-passage/))
- **Public Election Fund (PEF) opt-out.** Friedson is the only major candidate in the 2026 executive race **not** participating in the county's public financing system; he is using traditional fundraising and reportedly has a commanding cash advantage. Critics say this contradicts his good-government brand; he argues PEF caps would handicap a competitive county-wide campaign. ([Maryland Bay News](https://mdbaynews.com/2026/01/31/montgomery-county-executive-race-2026-candidates/))
- **Police in schools (April 28, 2026 letter).** Two days before a shooting at Blake High, Friedson publicly asked MCPS and county police to plan permanent officer assignments. Defenders point to the timing as prescient; critics point out the SRO program was scrapped after data showed disproportionate Black/Hispanic student arrests, and a CEO was already on-site at Blake. ([Bethesda Magazine](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/04/30/mcps-police-officers-in-schools/))
- **Endorsements:** IAFF Local 1664 (firefighters — first labor endorsement of the primary, July 2025); Councilmembers Balcombe and Luedtke (April 23, 2026); former candidate David Blair; Greater Greater Washington. ([Montgomery Perspective — IAFF](https://montgomeryperspective.com/2025/07/11/fire-fighters-endorse-friedson/), [GGWash endorsement](https://ggwash.org/view/102458/andrew-friedson-for-montgomery-county-executive))

## 6. Stance on listener-flagged topics

- **Sanctuary policy on illegal immigration.** Co-sponsored both the Trust Act (Bill 32-25) and the County Values Act (Bill 3-26). He is **not** a candidate who opposes sanctuary-style protections. If the listener wants someone on the ballot who would *roll back* those protections, none of the three leading Democrats (Friedson, Glass, Jawando) fit; all three are co-sponsors of County Values.
- **School funding.** Yes-vote on FY25 (99.2% of MCPS request) and FY26 (99.8%), both without a property-tax hike. Will not raise taxes to fund schools beyond what the existing tax base supports — listener should know this is a principle, not a one-time stance.
- **Property tax.** Voted no on every Elrich-proposed property-tax increase (2020, 2023, 2025, 2026). Of the three top Democrats, Friedson is the most consistent "no" on tax hikes.
- **Wootton High School.** On record opposing the Crown relocation; called for full renovation in October 2025 testimony. As county executive, he would not directly reverse the Board of Education's vote, but he would shape the capital budget that funds (or doesn't fund) renovation work at the existing site.
- **Fiscal discipline.** This is his core brand. The legislative record supports it on tax votes. The complication is that "fiscal discipline" *plus* "fully fund schools" *plus* "expand affordable housing" is a hard triangle that the next executive will have to defend in actual budgets — open question whether Friedson can deliver all three when he's the one signing the budget instead of voting on it.
