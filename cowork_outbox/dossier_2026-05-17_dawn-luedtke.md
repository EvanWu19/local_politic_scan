The name is uncommon enough in Maryland politics that disambiguation is straightforward; a separate Dawn Luedtke obituary appears in unrelated NJ death records [src: https://www.fissbillspoklasnyfuneralhome.com/obituaries/Dawn-Luedtke-35630/], but the Council Member is alive, sitting, and actively campaigning for re-election. The marriage is publicly known and disclosed [src: https://rivetingwomen.com/dawn-luedtke-insists-our-kids-deserve-safe-schools-and-walkable-communities/].

Public footprint thickens once she joined the AG office. Highlights:

This is a real specialist talk, not a press appearance.

## 5. Controversies & notable press

- **Bill 32-23 / Policing Advisory Commission rollback attempt (2023)** — Luedtke initially proposed repealing the Policing Advisory Commission. The Silver Spring Justice Coalition and the Montgomery County NAACP publicly opposed the repeal; she withdrew and substituted a name-change-and-restructure bill, which itself was amended to roll back provisions critics said would have reduced participation by overpoliced communities [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2023/10/24/montgomery-county-council-changes-name-of-policing-advisory-commission-clarifies-role/]. Framing: this is a documented evolution under public pressure, not a scandal.
- **Spousal-conflict optics** — Eric Luedtke is the chief legislative officer for Governor Moore. When the Council weighs state-mandated obligations (juvenile justice, education funding formulas, transportation aid) the spousal connection is salient. The dossier finds no documented ethics finding against her on this; it remains an optics question voters can weigh themselves.
- **No identified ethics complaints, FEC complaints, lawsuits, or news investigations** in the records reviewed.

If opposing sanctuary policy is the listener's primary issue, the answer is consistent with the broader Democratic primary field this season — none of the major Democratic incumbents in Montgomery County are running against sanctuary policy. The contrast is in the Republican primary or November ballot.
