## 1. RESOLVING THE PERSON

The person is **Laurie-Anne Sayles**, a Democratic At-Large member of the Montgomery County Council. She filed for re-election in 2026 and is the only sitting At-Large incumbent in the field. The Run for Something candidate directory, the Maryland State Archives manual entry, her county-government bio, and her campaign site (sayles4moco.com) all describe the same individual [src: directory.runforsomething.net/candidate/2457/sayles-laurie-anne ; src: msa.maryland.gov/msa/mdmanual/36loc/mo/leg/html/msa18474.html ; src: sayles4moco.com].

There is also a federal 2024 candidate filing under the same name — she ran in the Democratic primary for Maryland's 6th Congressional District in 2024 and lost. Same person [src: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Maryland's_6th_congressional_district_election].

## 3. DEEP NEWS HISTORY (≈2006–2026, i.e. age 25 to age 45)

- **2017:** Won election to Gaithersburg City Council, becoming the first Black/African-American elected to that body. Won the City of Gaithersburg Environmental Steward of the Year Award the same year [src: montgomerycountymd.gov/sayles/about.html].
- **2018:** Recognized as one of The Daily Record's "Top 100 Women in Maryland" [src: montgomerycountymd.gov/sayles/about.html].
- **2020:** Elevated to Vice President of the Gaithersburg City Council. Won the Women in Municipal Government (WIMG) award. Press coverage credits her with shepherding racial-equity legislation in Gaithersburg requiring racial-equity analysis before council votes — "spearheaded the groundbreaking racial equity legislation" [src: montgomerycountymd.gov/sayles/about.html].
- **2022:** Ran for Montgomery County Council At-Large in the open-field cycle after the council expanded from nine to eleven seats. Finished in the top four of the four At-Large seats in the November 2022 general; inaugurated December 5, 2022 [src: ballotpedia.org/Laurie-Anne_Sayles].
- **2023:** Honored with the Enhance Our Environment Award from the Xi Sigma Omega Chapter of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Inc.; and the Distinguished Advocate for Healthful Living Award from SHAPE Maryland [src: montgomerycountymd.gov/sayles/about.html].
- **2023–2024:** Lead sponsor with Councilmember Will Jawando of Expedited Bill 23-24, extending the County's Minority, Female, and Disabled-Owned Business Purchasing Program sunset to 2029 [src: montgomerycountymd.gov/Press_Detail.aspx?Item_ID=46008].
- **April 2024 / May 14, 2024:** Ran in the Democratic primary for Maryland's 6th Congressional District after Rep. David Trone vacated the seat to run for U.S. Senate. Lost the primary; April McClain-Delaney won with 39.1% and Joe Vogel finished second at 26.7%. Sayles was outside the top two [src: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Maryland's_6th_congressional_district_election ; src: elections.maryland.gov/elections/2024/primary_results/gen_results_2024_3_6.html]. Her campaign emphasized public-health credentials and her "S.M.A.R.T. Solutions" framework.
- **October–November 2024:** Continued as council member through and after the congressional loss; cosponsored Bill 18-24 (Housing Policy – Standards and Procedures Amendments) streamlining affordable-housing project approval [src: bethesdamagazine.com/2024/11/14/county-launches-grant-program-for-businesses-looking-to-expand-office-space/].
- **2024:** Sponsored the Make Office Vacancy Extinct (MOVE) Grant Program alongside Councilmembers Evan Glass, Natali Fani-González, and Marilyn Balcombe through the Economic Development Committee [src: bethesdamagazine.com/2024/11/14/county-launches-grant-program-for-businesses-looking-to-expand-office-space/].
- **2024:** Received the Outstanding Public Service Award from the Korean Association of Maryland and the President's Lifetime Achievement Award from the U.S. India SME Council, Inc. [src: montgomerycountymd.gov/sayles/about.html].
- **Early 2025:** Appointed Chair of the council's Health and Human Services Committee; launched a "Countywide Health and Human Services Tour" [src: montgomerycountymd.gov/Press_Detail.aspx?Item_ID=48015].
- **2025:** Cosponsored Council legislation accelerating office-to-housing conversions, led by Friedson and Fani-González [src: montgomerycountymd.gov/Press_Detail.aspx?Item_ID=46855].
- **2026 cycle:** Filed for re-election to the At-Large seat. Endorsed by Greater Greater Washington, MCEA (Apple Ballot), JUFJ Campaign Fund, Progressive Neighbors, and Governor Wes Moore [src: ggwash.org/view/102768 ; src: mceanea.org/2026-apple-ballot-local-endorsement/ ; src: jufjcampaignfund.org/montgomery/ ; src: progressiveneighborsmd.com/endorsements/ ; src: bethesdamagazine.com/2026/05/11/moore-endorses-moco-council-general-assembly-candidates/]. Greater Greater Washington noted she is "the only incumbent running for at-large, wrapping up her first term."
