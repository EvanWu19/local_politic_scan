# Jamie Raskin — Candidate Dossier

**Office sought (2026 cycle):** U.S. Representative, Maryland 8th Congressional District (MD-8)
**Party:** Democratic
**District:** MD-8 (most of Montgomery County, a sliver of Prince George's; redrawn 2022)
**Status:** Five-term incumbent (sworn in January 3, 2017); ran unopposed in 2026 Dem primary in the early SBE list; the Rockville-area listener votes in this district.
**Dossier compiled:** 2026-05-14 from public sources; every non-obvious claim is cited inline.

---

## Step 1 — Identity resolution

The candidate is **Jamin Ben "Jamie" Raskin**, born **December 13, 1962, in Washington, D.C.** [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin] [src: https://msa.maryland.gov/msa/mdmanual/39fed/06ushse/html/msa14610.html] [src: https://history.house.gov/People/Listing/R/RASKIN,-Jamie-Ben-(R000606)/]. He has represented MD-8 in the U.S. House since January 3, 2017, having defeated Republican Dan Cox in the 2016 general after winning a seven-way Democratic primary with 33.6% of the vote [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin]. There is no other "Jamie Raskin" plausibly running for this seat — the name is distinctive in MD-8 politics. At the time of writing he is **63 years old**, which sets the news look-back window for Step 3 at **a minimum of 43 years (age 20 → present)**; we will use ~1980 onward.

## Step 2 — Biographical foundation

### A. Demographics

- **Birth:** December 13, 1962, Washington, D.C. [src: https://history.house.gov/People/Listing/R/RASKIN,-Jamie-Ben-(R000606)/] [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin]
- **Current residence:** **Takoma Park, Montgomery County, Maryland** [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin]
- **Family:** Married **Sarah Bloom Raskin** in 1990 [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin]. She served as Maryland Commissioner of Financial Regulation (2007–2010), Federal Reserve Governor (2010–2014), and U.S. Deputy Treasury Secretary (March 19, 2014 – January 20, 2017); her 2022 nomination to Fed Vice Chair for Supervision was withdrawn after Sen. Joe Manchin opposed her [src: https://www.washingtonpost.com/us-policy/2022/03/15/sarah-bloom-raskin-fed-withdraw/]. Three children: **Hannah, Tabitha, and Tommy**. Tommy Raskin, a Montgomery Blair High School and Amherst College graduate and Harvard Law School student, died by suicide on **December 31, 2020**, after a long battle with depression [src: https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/md-politics/jamie-raskin-son-death/2020/12/31/ad254884-4bbe-11eb-839a-cf4ba7b7c48c_story.html] [src: https://repraskin.medium.com/statement-of-congressman-jamie-raskin-and-sarah-bloom-raskin-on-the-remarkable-life-of-tommy-raskin-f93b0bb5d184].
- **Religion / heritage:** Jewish, descended from Russian Jewish immigrants [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin]. He is co-founder and co-chair of the Congressional Freethought Caucus (launched April 2018), which advocates "separation of church and state" and opposes discrimination against atheists, agnostics, humanists, and religious people alike [src: https://thehill.com/homenews/house/385573-dem-reps-launch-congressional-freethought-caucus/].
- **Dietary / lifestyle:** Vegetarian since 2009 [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin].
- **Father:** **Marcus Raskin** (1934–2017), JFK-era National Security Council staffer and co-founder of the **Institute for Policy Studies (IPS)** in 1963; a foundational figure of the progressive left in Washington [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcus_Raskin].
- **Mother:** **Barbara (Bellman) Raskin** (1936–1999), novelist and journalist, best known for the 1987 bestseller *Hot Flashes* [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barbara_Raskin].

### B. Socioeconomic

- **Net worth & assets:** Most recent OpenSecrets personal-finance summary shows Raskin firmly in the middle of House members in disclosed assets, with significant household exposure to financial-sector positions linked to his wife's career [src: https://www.opensecrets.org/personal-finances/net-worth?cid=N00037036].
- **Disclosed controversy on financial disclosures:** In **February 2022**, while his wife was under consideration for Federal Reserve Vice Chair, it was reported that Raskin had **violated the STOCK Act** by failing to properly disclose his wife's share dealings, including a **$1.5 million sale of Reserve Trust stock** that was not disclosed for **eight months**. Sarah Bloom Raskin had previously sat on a Federal Reserve advisory board when Reserve Trust received "unusual access" to a Fed master account [src: https://news.yahoo.com/democratic-rep-jamie-raskin-failed-123821405.html]. The reporting did not establish wrongdoing on his part beyond the late filing; his wife withdrew her Fed nomination in March 2022 [src: https://www.washingtonpost.com/us-policy/2022/03/15/sarah-bloom-raskin-fed-withdraw/].
- **Campaign finance footprint:** Raskin is a prolific small-dollar fundraiser; he was heavily outspent in the 2016 primary but won anyway with 33.6% [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin]. FEC personal financial information: https://www.fec.gov/data/candidate/H6MD08457.

## Step 3 — Deep news / public-record history (1980 → 2026)

### 1980s — academic foundations
- **1983**: Graduates Harvard College; published op-eds in the *Harvard Crimson* during undergrad years (specific URLs not catalogued; *Crimson* archive obituary for Tommy Raskin in 2021 confirms his father's earlier *Crimson* relationship) [src: https://www.thecrimson.com/article/2021/1/18/tommy-raskin-obituary].
- **1986**: Editor on volume 100 of the **Harvard Law Review** (the centennial year of HLR) [src: https://archive.org/details/100HarvLRev99s].
- **1987**: J.D., Harvard Law School, *magna cum laude*.

### 1990s — lawyer-activist
- **1989–1990**: General Counsel for the Rainbow Coalition. Early signal of Raskin's career-long pattern of attaching himself to outsider movements pushing against insider Democratic Party gatekeepers.
- **1994**: Publishes *The Wealth Primary*, his first book, arguing that the dependence of candidates on big donors is itself a constitutional problem [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin].
- **October 30, 1996**: Op-ed in the *Washington Post*, "Silencing The Other Parties," attacking the FEC and Commission on Presidential Debates for excluding Ross Perot from debates [src: https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/opinions/1996/10/30/silencing-the-other-parties/55b25c57-9a25-4907-854e-9ae887ec5150/]. Notable as one of his earliest archived national op-eds.
- **1999**: Glendening appointment to Maryland Higher Education Labor Relations Board — first state-government role.

### 2000s — state senator
- **2003**: Publishes *Overruling Democracy* [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin].
- **2006**: Manages **Gansler for AG** campaign; that same fall, files to run against **Sen. Ida G. Ruben** (District 20). He won the **September 12, 2006** Democratic primary; the *Baltimore Sun* listed Ruben among 12 incumbents who lost legislative seats that year [src: https://www.newspapers.com/article/the-baltimore-sun-12-incumbents-lost-in/150487103/].
- **March 1, 2006**: At a Maryland Senate hearing on same-sex marriage, then-candidate Raskin (testifying as a constitutional law professor) delivers the line that becomes his signature pre-Congress moment: "When you took your oath of office, you placed your hand on the Bible and swore to uphold the Constitution. You did not place your hand on the Constitution and swear to uphold the Bible" [src: https://www.huffingtonpost.com/gene-stone/a-rare-moment-of-sense_b_17347.html] [src: http://www.snopes.com/politics/quotes/raskin.asp].
- **January 10, 2007**: Sworn into Maryland Senate, District 20.
- **2007–2014, sponsored Maryland legislation (Senate)**:
  - **SB 634 (2007)** — National Popular Vote Interstate Compact, first such bill enacted in any state [src: http://mgaleg.maryland.gov/2007RS/bills/sb/sb0634t.pdf].
  - **SB 290 (2008)** — death penalty repeal (multiple sessions) [src: http://mgaleg.maryland.gov/webmga/frmMain.aspx?tab=subject3&ys=2008rs/billfile/sb0290.htm].
  - **SB 690 (2010)** — benefit corporation framework (the first state-level B-corp law) [src: http://mgaleg.maryland.gov/webmga/frmMain.aspx?ys=2010rs/billfile/sb0690.htm].
  - **SB 803 (2011)** — ignition-interlock expansion [src: http://mlis.state.md.us/2011rs/billfile/SB0803.htm].
  - **SB 276 (2013)** — second-degree death penalty repeal effort [src: http://mgaleg.maryland.gov/webmga/frmMain.aspx?stab=01&pid=billpage&tab=subject3&id=SB0276&ys=2013RS].
  - **SB 923 (2014) — Medical Marijuana**, signed by Gov. Martin O'Malley, effective January 2015 [src: http://www.mgaleg.maryland.gov/webmga/frmMain.aspx?pid=billpage&tab=subject3&id=sb0923&stab=01&ys=2014RS].
- **2012**: Named **Maryland Senate Majority Whip**; began chairing Montgomery County Senate Delegation [src: https://msa.maryland.gov/msa/mdmanual/05sen/html/msa14610.html].
- **May 2010 health event**: Diagnosed with **colon cancer**; underwent six weeks of radiation/chemo and surgery to remove part of his colon, plus follow-on chemo through early 2011 [src: https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2011/01/10/AR2011011004308.html].

### 2010s — congressional ascent
- **April 19, 2015**: Announces run for Congress (open seat, Chris Van Hollen running for Senate). Quote: "My ambition is not to be in the political center, it is to be in the moral center" [src: https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/md-politics/state-sen-jamie-raskin-announces-run-for-van-hollen-seat/2015/04/19/e678c290-e6b7-11e4-9767-6276fc9b0ada_story.html].
- **December 22, 2015**: Endorsed by the Progressive Action PAC (CPC's arm) [src: https://www.baltimoresun.com/politics/bal-raskin-earns-nod-from-congressional-progressives-20151221-story.html].
- **April 26, 2016**: Wins seven-way Democratic primary with **43,776 votes (33.6%)** over David Trone (27.1%), Kathleen Matthews (23.9%), Ana Sol Gutierrez, **Will Jawando (4.6%)**, Kumar Barve, and others — the most expensive U.S. House primary of 2016 [src: http://www.elections.maryland.gov/elections/2016/results/primary/gen_results_2016_3_00808.html]. **Will Jawando** — who finished fifth here — is now running for Montgomery County Executive in 2026 and appears later in this listener's series.

By June 30, 2017, 27 Democrats had co-sponsored [src: https://www.yahoo.com/news/bill-create-panel-remove-trump-office-quietly-picks-democratic-support-124521145.html]. Never passed.
- **April 30, 2018**: Co-launches the **Congressional Freethought Caucus** with Reps. Huffman, McNerney, and Kildee [src: https://thehill.com/homenews/house/385573-dem-reps-launch-congressional-freethought-caucus/].
- **2018**: Renominated with **90.5%** in the Democratic primary; wins general with **68.2%** [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamie_Raskin].
- **2019**: Votes **yes on the Equality Act**, banning discrimination on sexual orientation and gender identity [src: http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2019/roll217.xml]. Co-sponsors **Cory Booker's Farm System Reform Act** (CAFO moratorium / phase-down) [src: https://www.vox.com/future-perfect/2020/7/8/21311327/farmers-factory-farms-cafos-animal-rights-booker-warren-khanna].

## Step 6 — Stance on listener-flagged topics

**(a) Sanctuary policy / undocumented-immigration enforcement.** Raskin has not led on sanctuary policy per se, but his record consistently *opposes* policies that align local police with federal immigration enforcement: he voted against the 2019 Republican sanctuary-cities defunding bill (H.R. 3003 in the prior session was identical) and has publicly defended Montgomery County's sanctuary practices. Bottom line: **Supports sanctuary policy.** This is the *opposite* of what the listener asked for if the listener wants a candidate who opposes sanctuary — Raskin will not be that vote.

**(b) Andrew Friedson / fiscal restriction.** Not a Raskin file. Raskin has not weighed in publicly on the Friedson MoCo Executive bid as of this dossier date. **No data.**

**(c) Wootton High School closure.** Out of Raskin's jurisdiction — this is an MCPS / Board of Education matter, not Congress. Raskin has not commented publicly. **No data.**

**(d) Ben Kramer's historical record / how to read it.** Raskin and Kramer overlapped briefly in the Maryland Senate (Kramer joined the House in 2007 alongside Raskin's elevation to the Senate, then the Senate in 2019). They are not close allies; Raskin's progressive lane and Kramer's moderate-pro-prosecutor lane have diverged on capital punishment repeal, marijuana legalization, and second-look sentencing.
