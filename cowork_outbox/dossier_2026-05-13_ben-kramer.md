## Step 1 — Resolving the person

The "Ben Kramer" running for re-election to Maryland State Senate District 19 in 2026 is **Benjamin F. Kramer** — full first name Benjamin, middle initial F. — born March 5, 1957 in Wheaton, Maryland [src: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_F._Kramer]. He is the son of the late Sidney Kramer (former Maryland state senator and Montgomery County Executive) and Betty Mae Kramer; his sister is Rona Kramer, who served as a state senator and as Maryland Secretary of Aging from 2015 to 2023 [src: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_F._Kramer]. He has served in the Maryland Senate since January 9, 2019; before that he served in the Maryland House of Delegates from January 10, 2007 through the start of the 2019 session — twelve years in the House and now approaching the end of his second Senate term [src: mgaleg.maryland.gov/mgawebsite/members/details/kramer02; ballotpedia.org/Benjamin_Kramer]. He is **not** to be confused with the actor of the same name or with any other Benjamin Kramer in U.S. politics; the District 19 candidate's official campaign site is **benkramer.org** and his MGA member ID page is **kramer02** [src: benkramer.org; mgaleg.maryland.gov/mgawebsite/members/details/kramer02].

**Year of birth (for step-3 lookback):** 1957. **Age at primary (June 23, 2026):** 69. **News lookback window = (69 − 20) = 49 years**, i.e., approximately 1977 forward.

## Step 3 — Deep news history (window 1977 forward, ~49-year lookback)

Kramer's news footprint, despite the wide lookback window, is concentrated in three eras: (a) the family-political-dynasty era of the 1980s and 90s (largely his father Sidney's coverage, with Ben only occasionally named), (b) his 2007-2019 House tenure (signature criminal-justice and aging-protection legislation, plus the Noah's Law package and the BDS bill), and (c) his Senate tenure from 2019 onward (Holocaust-education law, alcohol-control fights with Comptroller Franchot, and the March 2026 ethics-review episode involving Maryland First Lady Dawn Moore).

**a) 1979 – 2007 (pre-elective era).** No substantial named press coverage of Ben Kramer himself is sourced for this period. Father Sidney Kramer is regularly covered as state senator and County Executive; Ben is occasionally identified as "the son of County Executive Kramer," but no signed Ben Kramer op-eds, interviews, or public-record items earlier than his 2006 House campaign are surfaced in this round.

**b) 2006 – 2018: House of Delegates years.** Kramer ran for the Maryland House of Delegates in District 19 in 2006, winning the Democratic primary on September 12, 2006 with 14 percent of the vote and edging Paul Griffin by 307 votes for the third (last) nomination [src: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_F._Kramer]. He was sworn in January 10, 2007. Committee assignments: **Judiciary Committee 2007-2010**, then **Economic Matters Committee 2011-2019**.

Key House-era legislative work:

- **Noah's Law (Drunk Driving Reduction Act of 2016)** — Kramer was the lead House sponsor of HB 1342 / SB 945, named for Officer Noah A. Leotta of the Montgomery County Police Department, who was killed by a drunk driver during a 2015 holiday DUI checkpoint. The law made ignition-interlock installation mandatory even for first-offense DUI/DWI convictions. State Sen. Jamie Raskin (then D-20) carried the Senate companion. Kramer and Raskin together approached the Leotta family for permission to use Noah's name on the bill [src: wtop.com/maryland/2016/04/push-to-pass-noahs-law-down-to-the-wire-in-maryland/; cnsmaryland.org/2016/02/10/state-lawmakers-officers-family-lobby-for-all-offender-ignition-interlock-law-in-maryland/; baltimoresun.com/2016/09/30/mandatory-ignition-interlock-among-new-maryland-laws/]. Kramer publicly describes Noah's Law as "the strongest drunk driving legislation in the nation" [src: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_F._Kramer]. A 2016 Washington Post account documents the floor fight over a Senate-committee weakening amendment and the eventual House restoration [src: washingtonpost.com/local/md-politics/in-maryland-concern-that-noahs-law-has-been-watered-down/2016/03/09/98f36490-e62e-11e5-a6f3-21ccdbc5f74e_story.html].

- **Anti-BDS legislation (2017).** Kramer introduced a bill in the 2017 session to prohibit Maryland from contracting with companies that support the Boycott, Divestment, Sanctions movement against Israel. The bill failed; **Gov. Larry Hogan then signed an executive order codifying the policy** [src: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_F._Kramer]. The bill drew an unusual rebuke: **Archbishop Desmond Tutu issued a public statement of "grave concern"** over the legislation and over Kramer's on-record comparison between BDS supporters and Nazi Germany [src: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_F._Kramer]. This is the most internationally noticed item on Kramer's legislative record.

- **Senior-fraud / elder-protection package, 2007 forward.** Kramer's biographical summary credits him with making Maryland "the undisputed leader in the nation in providing strong and effective laws to protect our seniors from the con artists and scammers." Specific bills include vulnerable-adult financial-exploitation criminal-penalty bills. Specific bill numbers and years are present in the MGA bill database; this dossier round confirms the policy-area attribution but does not transcribe every bill number. **Open question** to enumerate the specific bill numbers next pass [src: benkramer.org/about-ben/; mgaleg.maryland.gov/mgawebsite/members/details/kramer02].

**c) 2019 – present: Senate years.**

- **2017 announcement and 2018 election.** In August 2017, Kramer announced he would run for the open District 19 Senate seat that Roger Manno was vacating to run for Congress, after briefly considering a run for County Executive [src: bethesdamagazine.com/Bethesda-Beat/2017/Kramer-will-pursue-open-District-19-Senate-seat-rather-than-county-executive/]. He ran unopposed in the 2018 Democratic primary and defeated Green Party nominee David Jeang in the November 6, 2018 general election. Sworn in January 9, 2019 [src: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_F._Kramer; ballotpedia.org/Benjamin_Kramer].

- **Holocaust education law (2019-2020).** Kramer introduced legislation requiring the state to develop guidelines for middle and high schools to teach the Holocaust and other contemporary acts of genocide. The bill died in committee in 2019. Kramer then wrote a letter to the Maryland State Department of Education pressing the agency to act administratively. In October 2019, State Superintendent Karen Salmon announced a tightening of Holocaust-education requirements; the Jewish Community Relations Council of Greater Washington publicly thanked Kramer by name as a "key legislator" [src: marylandmatters.org/2019/10/23/md-to-expand-holocaust-instruction-in-schools/; baltimoresun.com/education/bs-md-holocaust-education-20190717-pl4f6cyf5jefvigob22rt4bhm4-story.html; jcouncil.org/statements/maryland-jewish-advocacy-organizations-and-key-legislators-applaud-state-superintendents].

- **Franchot ethics fight (February 2019).** During testimony before the Senate Education, Health and Environmental Affairs Committee on a bill to shift alcohol regulation away from the Office of the Comptroller, Kramer accused Comptroller Peter Franchot of "extorting" hundreds of thousands of dollars in campaign contributions from liquor interests and likened him to Bernie Madoff. Franchot's chief of staff called the comments "beyond offensive" and "slanderous"; Franchot's office filed an ethics complaint with the Joint Committee on Legislative Ethics [src: washingtonpost.com/local/md-politics/comptroller-franchots-office-files-ethics-complaint-against-sen-kramer/2019/02/25/25c14006-38ff-11e9-a2cd-307b06d0257b_story.html; marylandmatters.org/2019/02/25/franchot-to-file-ethics-complaint-against-senator-following-explosive-accusation-at-hearing/; moco360.media/2019/02/25/maryland-comptroller-to-file-ethics-complaint-against-montgomery-lawmaker/]. Franchot in turn flagged that Kramer and his sister Rona Kramer own a building leased to the county-run Department of Liquor Control for roughly $20,000 a month [src: bethesdamagazine.com/2019/02/05/franchot-kramer-trade-sharp-jabs-over-roles-with-alcohol-businesses-tobacco/]. The outcome of the ethics complaint was never publicly resolved; Kramer characterized the complaint as "deflect and distract."

- **2022 primary challenge.** Zachary Kiser, a 31-year-old Aspen Hill resident, challenged Kramer in the 2022 Democratic primary, arguing Kramer was out of touch and insufficiently progressive [src: marylandmatters.org/2021/05/13/montgomery-county-sen-kramer-gets-democratic-primary-challenge/]. Kramer won the July 19, 2022 primary and defeated Anita Cox (Republican) and David Jeang (Green) in the November 8, 2022 general election [src: ballotpedia.org/Benjamin_Kramer].

- **March 2026 ethics review — Dawn Moore comments.** During a Senate Finance Committee hearing on a bill to ban the retail sale of kratom and similar products, Maryland First Lady Dawn Moore testified in support. Kramer commented on Moore's physical appearance and added "if you ever get bored with the governor … I'm just putting it out there." Kramer later said the remarks were intended as light-hearted humor. Senate President Bill Ferguson publicly condemned the comments as inappropriate and confirmed the Joint Committee on Legislative Ethics had "an opportunity to review" them; the committee's work is confidential by statute unless it elects public action [src: marylandmatters.org/2026/03/27/bouchat-gets-charitable-ellis-gets-angry-kramer-gets-reviewed-in-political-notes/; en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_F._Kramer]. This is the most recent on-record controversy at the time of this dossier compilation.

- **2026 re-election filing.** On February 3, 2026, Kramer filed for re-election as part of a District 19 slate with Dels. Charlotte Crutchfield and Vaughn Stewart. (Del. Bonnie Cullison is vacating her seat to run for County Council, creating the open delegate spot that has drawn Alec Stone and others into the race.) [src: bethesdamagazine.com/2026/02/03/kramer-crutchfield-stewart-file-reelection/]

**Prior runs for office:** Won — 2006 House primary; 2006, 2010, 2014 House general elections; 2018 Senate general; 2022 Senate primary and general. Lost — none on the public record.

**Party switches:** None. Lifelong Democrat.

## Step 6 — Stance on listener-flagged topics

**a) Sanctuary policy / opposition to local-ICE cooperation.**
Kramer has not made a high-profile floor speech opposing Maryland's "sanctuary" trajectory. He represents a district with strong immigrant-community presence (parts of Wheaton, Aspen Hill, Glenmont, Rockville) and has not publicly broken with the Senate Democratic caucus on the 2025 watered-down Maryland immigration bill that prohibited some 287(g) cooperation in "sensitive locations" (schools, hospitals, churches) [src: marylandmatters.org/2025/05/30/homeland-security-labels-maryland-several-counties-and-cities-sanctuary-jurisdictions/]. **No public statement is sourced in which Kramer explicitly opposes sanctuary-jurisdiction status.** **Bottom line: there is no public record of Kramer opposing Maryland's sanctuary direction.** If the listener is looking for a Democratic primary candidate who openly opposes sanctuary policy, Kramer is not that candidate.

**b) Friedson and fiscal restraint comparison.**
Andrew Friedson (County Council District 1) is running for County Executive, not for Senate District 19, so he is not on Kramer's ballot line. Friedson's fiscal-restraint reputation is built on his county-council work, particularly opposition to the Office of Racial Equity and Social Justice (ORESJ) funding levels, push for an independent inspector-general for MCPS, and tax-rate-position skepticism on the FY26 budget. **Kramer's own fiscal record** is harder to pigeonhole: in committee, he has favored increased Medicaid managed-care reimbursement, expanded senior-protection enforcement funding, and a heavier hand on alcohol-tax / sin-tax revenue. He is not a publicly identified fiscal hawk. **Bottom line: if "fiscal restraint" is the listener's filter, Kramer is closer to the middle-of-the-Democratic-caucus mainstream than to Friedson's restraint posture.**

**c) Wootton High School closure.**
The MCPS Board of Education voted 7-1 on **March 26, 2026** to approve Modified Option H, which moves the Wootton population into the new Crown High School building in Gaithersburg and converts the existing Wootton plant into a holding school [src: thebanner.com/education/k-12-schools/mcps-boundary-program-votes-GVUO6O46LZHNPHTU26DTQMWIWM/; savewootton.org/the-issue/]. A community group (CEPA) filed an administrative appeal with the Maryland State Board of Education in late April 2026 [src: montgomeryperspective.com/2026/04/28/wootton-group-appeals-mcps-closure-of-wootton-high-school/]. **Wootton sits in District 39, not District 19** — but the boundary changes do touch District 19 attendance areas. **No public statement from Sen. Kramer specifically opposing or supporting the Wootton closure decision is sourced in this dossier round.** Sen. Cheryl Kagan (D-17) has been vocal against the closure; Kramer has not [src: savewootton.org/the-issue/]. **Bottom line: unclear — no on-record position located.** This is a fair on-air "we don't have a public record yet" admission and a fair listener-question-to-press item.

**d) "Full historical voting record including old news."**
This dossier compiles Kramer's signature legislative work (Noah's Law, Holocaust-education campaign, senior-fraud package, anti-BDS bill) but does **not** enumerate every individual roll-call vote across 19 years. A complete vote-by-vote record across the 2007-2026 sessions would require pulling the MGA bill-history data per bill and aggregating; that is feasible but is a separate project. **Open task** for a future dossier pass: roll up the full session-by-session yes/no record on (i) gun safety, (ii) reproductive rights, (iii) trans rights, (iv) tax policy, (v) energy / climate, (vi) housing policy.

**e) Side-by-side comparison with primary opponents.**
**As of the dossier compilation date, Kramer has no announced Democratic primary opponent for the June 23, 2026 Senate District 19 race.** The District 19 slate filed Feb. 3, 2026 names Kramer at the top of the ticket [src: bethesdamagazine.com/2026/02/03/kramer-crutchfield-stewart-file-reelection/].
