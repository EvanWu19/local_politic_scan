## 1. Resolved identity

Arthur Ellis, sitting Maryland State Senator from Charles County (District 28). First elected to the Senate in 2018; re-elected 2022. Retired U.S. Army officer (Lt. Colonel). The first Black senator from Charles County.

---

## 2. Biographical foundation

Born in Mississippi; raised in a military family. U.S. Army career (active duty and Reserve) spanning over twenty years; retired as Lieutenant Colonel. Master's degree in business administration. Settled in Charles County after military service.

---

## 3. Legislative record

**Committee assignments.** Education, Health, and Environmental Affairs (EHEA) Committee.

**Notable legislative work:**
- Co-sponsor on multiple veterans' health and benefits bills.
- Lead sponsor on a 2024 bill expanding tuition assistance for Maryland National Guard members.
- Voted yes on Senate Bill 245 (the 287(g) prohibition) in 2026.
- Voted yes on the Utility RELIEF Act in 2026.

**Floor votes on listener-priority issues:**
- Sanctuary policy: voted yes on the recent sanctuary-leaning bills.
- Education funding: aligned with the Senate Democratic majority on Blueprint funding.
- Tax/budget: aligned with the majority on the structural-deficit-closing package.

**Prior controversy.** In 2021 Ellis was the subject of a public dispute with then-Senate President Bill Ferguson over committee leadership and was briefly reassigned within the Democratic Caucus. The episode is documented in Maryland Matters coverage from 2021 and is not currently relevant to the 2026 cycle but is part of his full career record.
