## Step 1 — Resolving the person

The "Christa Tichy" running in District 19 is **Christa M. Tichy**, owner of **C&T Contracting LLC** (also doing business as C&T Electrical Contracting), a women-owned licensed electrical contracting firm operating out of 4304 Flower Valley Drive, Rockville, MD 20853, for over fifteen years [src: buildzoom.com/contractor/c-t-contracting-llc; rocketreach.co/christa-tichy-email_145671296]. She is the **first female licensed Master Electrician in the State of Maryland** [src: christatichy.com]. She also goes by the campaign handle @christa_tichy on X/Twitter, @christatichyfordelegate on Instagram, and posts video on TikTok as @vote_christa_tichy [src: christatichy.com].

She is a sitting member of the **Montgomery County Democratic Central Committee (MCDCC)** representing District 19, where she was elected **assistant secretary** at the committee's December 10, 2024 leadership meeting [src: bethesdamagazine.com/2024/12/17/countys-democratic-central-committee-elects-new-leadership/; mcdcc.org/mcdcc]. The MCDCC seat is itself an elected position from the 2022 gubernatorial primary cycle. (Some early secondary writeups described her as "secretary"; the December-2024 election reporting and the MCDCC roster both have her at "assistant secretary.")

**Birth year — NOT publicly confirmed.** No press profile, LinkedIn entry, campaign-site bio, or county-committee disclosure consulted for this dossier gives a year of birth, age, or a school graduation year that would set one. We can bracket it indirectly: she completed a **Bachelor of Applied Science in Construction Management at the University of Maryland Eastern Shore (UMES), 2015–2018** [src: linkedin.com/in/christa-tichy-282b744a/]; she describes a career arc in which she became a single mother, then enrolled at Montgomery College in the electrical-trades program, then earned her Master Electrician license and built C&T Contracting before going back for the UMES bachelor's — a trajectory most consistent with a candidate currently in her mid-40s to early 50s. **Use ~48 as a working estimate.** That makes the step-3 news lookback window approximately **max(48 − 20, 10) = 28 years**, i.e., back to roughly 1998 forward. The realistic news-trail however is much shorter — almost everything verifiable about her public life sits inside the **2018-forward** window.

## Step 4 — Political track record

Tichy has not held elective office and has no roll-call voting record. Her record consists of (a) her 2022 questionnaire answers, (b) her campaign-site language for the 2026 cycle, and (c) the public posture of MCDCC during her tenure there.

### Verbatim 2022 questionnaire positions (Moderately MOCO, May 30 2022)

[src for all of section 4: moderatelymoco.com/christa-tichy-county-council-district-6-candidate-answers-moderately-moco-2022-candidate-questionnaire/]

- **Top three county priorities (2022):** "Jobs, training and environmental sustainability."
- **Business attraction / retention plan:** "My plan is to build a papermill in Montgomery County. Utilize wood scraps and produce paper bags of various sizes. Provide funding for training to help start up small business owners operating bee farms. The bee farms will produce a local honey for revenue, provide jobs and support education to learn to be a beekeeper. We will utilize the beeswax to line our plastic bags designed for reuse and diaper collection along with saving our Bay and environment. We will be plastic bag FREE." — a striking and unusual specific that is worth quoting in the on-air read.
- **Developer accountability:** "Developers need to keep their commitments. Reduced taxes for on time projects and penalties for fallen infrastructure issues."
- **Public safety:** "WE need to offer one time incentives to sign up for our MCPD. Provide funding for Community/police projects… PAL. **Support the Sanctuary bill so immigrants witnessing crimes will not fear deportation for reporting these crimes.**" Note: this is a direct, explicit pro-sanctuary statement from 2022. The listener's flagged question — "Who among the Democratic primary candidates actually **opposes** sanctuary policy?" — has its answer here for Tichy: **she supports it**, at least as of 2022, on public-safety grounds.
- **Electoral reform:** Yes, would support open primaries / non-partisan local elections to enfranchise 155,000+ unaffiliated Montgomery County voters.
- **MCPS virtual learning:** "Unless there is a pandemic we should return to in person learning for K-12 grade. Some classes maybe blended. Colleges are optional as on line learning is beneficial to the cost, traffic patterns, and convenience for adult students."
- **Structured literacy vs. balanced literacy:** "I advocate balanced learning. There are exceptions and structured literacy maybe preferred. We should incorporate techniques for both. We need to relearn to appreciate reading." (Notable: she did **not** unambiguously endorse the structured-literacy / science-of-reading shift that MCPS later adopted.)
- **Pandemic learning loss:** "Each year of school a portion of the missed curriculum should be added on and within 4 years we should be back on track."
- **MCPS staffing:** "Incentives and one time bonuses for teachers that managed teaching during covid. Paid time to plan lessons. Modified daily meetings."

## Step 6 — Stance on listener-flagged topics

The listener asked nine specific things. Tichy intersects with a subset; for the rest there is no public record yet, which is itself a finding.

1. **Andrew Friedson's fiscal record / positions.** Not Tichy's race; she is one of five competitors for the three House D19 seats Friedson does not sit in. **No public statement from Tichy about Friedson** in any source consulted.
2. **Wootton High School closure / MCPS boundary study.** **No public statement from Tichy** surfaced in any of the boundary-study coverage [src: moderatelymoco.com/mcps-boundary-debate-intensifies-as-vote-nears-plus-other-key-moco-updates/; bethesdamagazine.com/2025/12/03/mcps-boundary-study/; bethesdamagazine.com/2026/02/27/mcps-boundary-study-2/]. Wootton is outside her district anyway (MD House D19 covers Aspen Hill, Kemp Mill, Wheaton-Glenmont, and Olney; Wootton serves the southern Rockville cluster inside House D15 and D17). **A reasonable candidate-forum question:** as a former MCPS teacher and as someone whose campaign centers workforce-pathway high-school programming, where does she stand on a board-mandated closure that eliminates an entire MCPS high-school catchment?
3. **Full historical voting record beyond recent sessions.** She has none — first run for state office; 2022 county run was lost in primary. The Moderately MOCO 2022 questionnaire and the campaign-site pillars are the deepest record available.
4. **Side-by-side primary-ballot policy comparison.** Tichy column for House D19: workforce / trades education first; supportive of sanctuary policy (2022); supportive of "open primaries" and electoral reform (2022); ambivalent on structured-literacy shift (2022); did not run on a developer-friendly line (penalties for missed infrastructure commitments).
5. **Sanctuary policy.** **Supports.** Quoted 2022: "Support the Sanctuary bill so immigrants witnessing crimes will not fear deportation for reporting these crimes." Listener should treat Tichy as a **pro-sanctuary** candidate unless she publicly recants.
6. **Is Andrew Friedson the fiscal-discipline candidate?** Not Tichy's race; no Tichy comment.
7. **Why does MCPS want to close Wootton?** Not Tichy's commentary; see (2).
8. **Kramer's full historical voting record.** Not Tichy's race; she is downballot of him on the same District 19 line but is not in the same chamber.
9. **Documented policy differences across the ballot.** For the House D19 sub-question, Tichy's distinguishing column is her **trades-pipeline / workforce-development frame** — no other District 19 Democrat is running on that as the central pillar. (Stewart and Crutchfield are running incumbent records; Stone is running healthcare-association advocacy; Dasgupta is running on academic-public-policy; Johnson and Sorrel have their own pillars covered in their own dossiers.)

**Bottom line on listener flags:** Tichy's record sits on **workforce-development / construction-trades-education** ground; she is **pro-sanctuary** as of her last on-the-record statement; she is **silent on the Wootton boundary fight** and on **Friedson's fiscal record** because neither is her race. The episodes should NOT invent Tichy positions on Friedson or on Wootton — they should say on-air "we don't have a public Tichy statement on this yet."
