# Candidate Dossier — Evan M. Glass

**Office sought:** Montgomery County Executive
**Party:** Democratic
**District:** countywide (Montgomery County, Maryland)
**Primary:** June 23, 2026
**Dossier compiled:** 2026-05-21

---

## Step 1 — Person resolution

The Evan Glass on the 2026 Democratic primary ballot for Montgomery County Executive is **Evan Matthew Glass**, a two-term At-Large member of the Montgomery County Council (2018–present) who lives in Silver Spring. He filed for County Executive on Sept. 9, 2025, after announcing his run on March 12, 2025. He qualified for Maryland's county-level Public Election Fund in May 2025, the fastest qualification of any County Executive candidate in the three election cycles the public-financing system has existed [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2025/09/09/glass-county-executive-files/], [src: https://www.evanglass.com/news/moco-show-evan-glass-qualifies-for-public-financing-in-montgomery-county-executive-race]. He is not the same as any of several other public figures named Evan Glass; the Maryland State Archives biographical page confirms identity, council seat, and date of birth [src: https://msa.maryland.gov/msa/mdmanual/36loc/mo/leg/html/msa17975.html].

**Estimated year of birth:** 1977 (Jan. 30, 1977 per his published birth date on biographical sources [src: https://en.everybodywiki.com/Evan_Glass]). Age on primary day 2026-06-23: **49**.

**News lookback window:** age − 20 = 29 years, so 1997–2026. We use 1997–2026 throughout.

## Step 2 — Biographical foundation

### A. Demographics
- **Born:** Jan. 30, 1977, Westbury, New York [src: https://en.everybodywiki.com/Evan_Glass].
- **Parents:** Mona Glass (bookkeeper) and David Glass (insurance underwriter); raised primarily by his single mother [src: https://en.everybodywiki.com/Evan_Glass], [src: https://www.evanglass.com/].
- **Current residence:** Silver Spring, Montgomery County, Maryland — he has lived in Montgomery County for roughly 26 years (since 1999) [src: https://www.evanglass.com/].
- **Family:** Married; husband's first name is Jason; the couple has two rescue beagles, Daisy and Poppy [src: https://www.montgomerycountymd.gov/glass/about.html].
- **Self-identified orientation:** Openly gay; came out publicly in 2000; was the first openly gay person elected to the Montgomery County Council, in 2018 [src: https://www.washingtonblade.com/2025/03/12/evan-glass-running-for-montgomery-county-executive/], [src: https://victoryfund.org/candidate/glass-evan/].

### B. Socioeconomic
- **Net worth:** No comprehensive published figure. He files annual Maryland State Ethics financial disclosures (required of all Montgomery County councilmembers) — the most recent public set lists his salary as a councilmember (~$144,000 base for an at-large councilmember per the County Council's published FY26 salary table [src: https://www.montgomerycountymd.gov/COUNCIL/]). No major business interests, real-estate holdings beyond his Silver Spring residence, or stock holdings have been reported by Bethesda Magazine, Maryland Matters, or the Washington Post in coverage of his campaign.

## Step 3 — Deep news history (1997–2026)

**1995–1999 — American University.** No surviving press hits on Glass from this era that this researcher could confirm.

**1999–2011 — CNN.** As a working journalist his name appears in CNN production credits rather than the news itself; he was not himself the subject of major news coverage during this period. Worth noting because some campaign opponents have framed him as a "lifelong politician" — the public record is that he spent his entire 20s and most of his 30s in journalism.

**2006–2012 — Civic emergence in Silver Spring.** He became visible locally through the Silver Spring Citizens Advisory Board and the South Silver Spring Neighborhood Association. Local blog Greater Greater Washington tracked his advocacy for converting a strip mall into a downtown Silver Spring urban park [src: https://ggwash.org/view/29523/silver-spring-strip-mall-could-become-new-urban-park].

**2012–2014 — Pride and the first run for council.** Glass spearheaded Silver Spring's first formal Pride observances during this period; he has said publicly that he received hate mail and threats for doing so [src: https://www.evanglass.com/news/i1l4yl60u1ohoc17esrsk3ixyt8a3v]. In 2014 he ran in the Democratic primary for the Council's District 5 seat, finishing a close second to Tom Hucker [src: https://www.theseventhstate.com/?tag=evan-glass].

**2014–2018 — Nonprofit period.** Ran Gandhi Brigade Youth Media. Continued community work; helped found additional civic-association initiatives [src: https://en.everybodywiki.com/Evan_Glass].

**2018 — Elected At-Large.** Ran for one of four at-large seats in a 30+ candidate Democratic primary; placed third in the June primary and first in the November general; took office Dec. 3, 2018. The win made him the first openly gay person ever elected to the Montgomery County Council [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2018/11/07/albornoz-glass-jawando-and-friedson-will-join-county-council/], [src: https://www.sourceofthespring.com/montgomery-county-news/178683/elrich-leading-blair-riemer-glass-jawando-albornoz-among-winners/].

**2019–2022 — First term legislation.** Lead-sponsored Bill 33-19 (Police — Community Policing, 2020) and follow-on Bill 45-20 (Police — Community Policing — Data, 2021), which required Montgomery County Police to publish certain interaction datasets on Data Montgomery [src: https://www.montgomerycountymd.gov/glass/legislative-work.html]. Co-introduced (with Sidney Katz) the **Housing Justice Act**, signed into law April 2021, limiting how landlords can use criminal-history and credit information against rental applicants [src: https://content.govdelivery.com/accounts/MDMONTGOMERY/bulletins/2cd4ca7?reqfrom=share].

**2022 — Re-elected.** Won an at-large seat outright in the November 2022 general election.

**2023–2025 — Second term legislative and leadership role.** Served as Council Vice President in 2023. Continued legislative emphasis on housing affordability, transit-oriented zoning, and public safety oversight. Authored a series of formal Pride proclamations and was Montgomery County's main public face for the county's annual Pride flag-raising in Rockville [src: https://www2.montgomerycountymd.gov/mcgportalapps/Press_Detail.aspx?Item_ID=47157].

**2026 — Immigration sequence and County Executive campaign.** Glass was the lead sponsor on the **ICE Out Act**, passed unanimously by the council in April 2026, which forbids Montgomery County from issuing building or use-and-occupancy permits for any privately operated immigration detention center inside the county [src: https://wjla.com/news/local/montgomery-county-passes-ice-out-legislation-block-immigration-detention-facilities-councilmember-evan-glass-immigration-customs-enforcement], [src: https://wtop.com/montgomery-county/2026/04/setting-an-example-montgomery-co-council-bans-private-immigration-detention-centers/]. Earlier in 2026 (February), he was part of the unanimous vote on the Trust Act, which bars the county from entering Section 287(g) cooperation agreements with ICE [src: https://wtop.com/montgomery-county/2026/02/montgomery-county-passes-bill-to-protect-immigrants-and-limit-cooperation-with-ice/]. In late April 2026 he introduced a separate bill requiring extra screening of Montgomery County Police Department applicants who have done DHS immigration enforcement work since Jan. 21, 2025 [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/04/27/glass-bill-would-add-screening-dhs-agents-join-moco-police/].

- **Bill 33-19** — Police — Community Policing (lead-sponsor; enacted 2020). Required updates to MCPD community-policing data.
- **Bill 45-20** — Police — Community Policing — Data (lead-sponsor; enacted 2021). Required public posting of police-stop datasets.
- **Bill 8-24 (Housing Justice Act follow-on)** — Human Rights & Civil Liberties — Fair Criminal History and Credit Screenings — Amendments [src: https://www2.montgomerycountymd.gov/mcgportalapps/Press_Detail.aspx?Item_ID=45627&amp=&Dept=1].
- **Housing Justice Act of 2020 (introduced)** — co-introduced Dec. 7, 2020 with Sidney Katz; passed April 20, 2021 [src: https://content.govdelivery.com/accounts/MDMONTGOMERY/bulletins/2cd4ca7?reqfrom=share].
- **Trust Act of 2026** — voted yes on unanimous passage February 2026 [src: https://wtop.com/montgomery-county/2026/02/montgomery-county-passes-bill-to-protect-immigrants-and-limit-cooperation-with-ice/].
- **ICE Out Act of 2026** — lead sponsor; passed unanimously April 2026 [src: https://www.montgomerycountymd.gov/news/montgomery-county-council-passes-ice-out-act].

### FY2027 budget vote (May 15, 2026)
Voted YES on the council's $7.9 billion FY27 operating budget — included a $143.7 million increase for MCPS, totaling roughly $3.7 billion for the school system [src: https://www.mymcmedia.org/council-votes-9-2-to-approve-7-9b-budget/], [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/05/15/county-council-budget-straw-approval/]. Voted **AGAINST** a separate motion to shift Capital Improvements Program dollars into the operating budget to save school jobs, citing the harm to facilities maintenance [src: https://www.mymcmedia.org/council-votes-9-2-to-approve-7-9b-budget/].

### Public position on this campaign's signature issues
- **Housing affordability** — pro-supply; favors zoning reforms to allow more housing near transit; cites his Housing Justice Act record [src: https://www.thebanner.com/politics-power/local-government/evan-glass-county-executive-candidate-HENG5GJITVA5RIVLG7F4LIRBBM/].
- **Schools** — supports the council's MCPS funding increase but is publicly arguing for **structural reform of the Board of Education**, including adding a County-Executive-appointed seat to strengthen fiscal accountability [src: https://www.montgomerycountymd.gov/news/councilmember-evan-glass-pushes-reforms-montgomery-county-school-board], [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/05/20/opinion-its-time-to-reform-the-moco-school-board/].
- **Trump administration** — has explicitly said opposing federal Trump-administration actions, especially in immigration and federal-worker layoffs, will be a "cornerstone" of his campaign [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2025/03/12/glass-announces-county-executive-run/].
- **Government services** — frames his pitch as "run the buses, teach the kids, pick up the trash" — operational competence over ideology [src: https://www.mymcmedia.org/glass-announces-run-for-county-executive/].

## Step 6 — Stance on listener-flagged topics

**Sanctuary policy / opposition to sanctuary frameworks.** Glass is at the **strongly pro-sanctuary** end of the field. He lead-sponsored the ICE Out Act and the DHS-screening bill, and voted for the Trust Act [src: https://www.montgomerycountymd.gov/news/montgomery-county-council-passes-ice-out-act], [src: https://wtop.com/montgomery-county/2026/02/montgomery-county-passes-bill-to-protect-immigrants-and-limit-cooperation-with-ice/]. **Bottom line:** OPPOSES any rollback of Montgomery County's sanctuary posture. If the listener is looking for a candidate who would relax sanctuary policy, Glass is not that candidate.

**Andrew Friedson's fiscal record vs. Glass.** Glass is not a fiscal-restraint candidate in the way Friedson positions himself. Glass voted YES on the FY27 budget including the $143.7M MCPS increase and against shifting CIP money to operations. Glass supports a progressive county income tax structure included in the FY27 budget agreement [src: https://www.montgomerycountymd.gov/news/montgomery-county-council-reaches-agreement-79-billion-fiscal-year-2027-operating-budget-focused-implementing-progressive-income-tax-structure-rejecting-proposed-property-tax-rate-increase-supporting-essential-services-residents]. **Bottom line:** Glass is a SPEND-FORWARD candidate paired with a structural tax fix, not a budget-cutter.

**Wootton High School closure.** We don't have a public record of Glass taking a specific position on Wootton's facility timeline. He has said operationally MCPS facilities are in poor shape — he cited his visit to New Hampshire Estates Elementary where he experienced respiratory issues, and used it to push back against shifting CIP money into the operating budget [src: https://www.mymcmedia.org/council-votes-9-2-to-approve-7-9b-budget/]. The decision on closing/relocating Wootton sits with the Board of Education, not the County Council. **Bottom line:** UNCLEAR specifically on Wootton; his Board of Education reform proposal (county-executive-appointed seat) would change who decides such matters going forward.

**Ben Kramer's full voting record.** Out of scope — Kramer is on the listener's ballot for State Senate D19 and gets his own dossier.

**Side-by-side ballot matrix.** Glass's row, summarized: sanctuary = strongly pro; fiscal = spend-forward + progressive income tax; MCPS spending = increase + structural BoE reform; Wootton = no public position.

**MCPS school rating system.** Glass has not taken a specific public position on the new MCPS school-rating system itself. He has called for **adding a County Executive-appointed seat to the elected Board of Education** to improve accountability and coordination on fiscal oversight, the very layer at which the rating system is administered [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/05/20/opinion-its-time-to-reform-the-moco-school-board/].
