JORDAN: Item two. What the County Executive role actually does. The County Executive of Montgomery County is the head of the executive branch of county government. The role is roughly analogous to the mayor of a large city, but the jurisdiction is a county of over one million residents and a budget of nearly eight billion dollars. The executive proposes the annual operating budget and the capital budget to the council, which then approves, amends, or rejects line items. The executive runs the county's executive departments, including the Department of Police, the Department of Transportation, the Department of Permitting Services, the Department of Health and Human Services, the Department of Recreation, the Department of Libraries, the Department of Environmental Protection, and so on. The executive does not run MCPS. MCPS is governed by the elected Board of Education, with the superintendent reporting to the Board. That is the structural reality. The County Executive shapes school spending by submitting a budget recommendation to the council, and the council then funds the Board of Education at the level it chooses. The executive cannot directly close a school. The Board does that.
