Per the dossier rule of `(candidate_age − 20)` years for the news look-back, the window is **~1990 to present (36 years)**, with childhood / teen years deliberately excluded.

Kumar Barve and J. D. Kumar are different people; Barve has held office in Maryland since 1991. Listeners should not confuse the two.

## Step 5 — Stated policy positions

These are drawn directly from votejdk.com [src: https://www.votejdk.com/] [src: https://www.votejdk.com/about]. They are the candidate's own framing; many use coined acronyms unique to his platform.

- **"$250 Billion Dollar Jobs Creation, Investments and Economic Activity Plan" for MD-8** — "$50 Billion Dollars per year over the next five years." No mechanism or pay-for is specified.
- **"$5 Trillion Dollar Society Decency. Civil Rights. Constitution Protections. 'Rule of Law' Plan,"** also called the **"Planetary $Trillions Plan (TPST Plan)"** — framed as a nationwide / global human-development push. No mechanism or pay-for is specified.
- **Mandatory 8 weeks of annual vacation for all workers** — "not a communism proposal, but a 21st century HDI improvement proposal."
- **K-10 education replacing K-12** — students enter work, mandatory government or private-sector service for two years (junior and senior year equivalent), then optional university; intended to reduce college loan burden and free up local property-tax pressure.
- **"Zero Confinement Policy Concept" (ZCPC)** — a long-horizon proposal to reduce incarceration for non-dangerous offenders, funded via the $250B plan; deliberately framed as multi-decade.
- **Border enforcement via heavy employer penalties + expanded H-2B seasonal visa program.** This is the candidate's stated approach to undocumented immigration — *not* sanctuary expansion. He frames sanctuary-friendly local Democrats negatively; see Step 6.
- **Hydroelectric expansion** — claims to be working with "North Sea consortiums" on hydro innovations and wants U.S. hydro to scale from "just over 5%" to "33%" of the grid. No partner firms named.
- **Trade orientation** — explicitly names Brazil, Canada, China, Mexico, the EU, the African Union (especially Commonwealth countries), and Korea/Japan as "preferred favorable nations" for low or mutual-zero tariffs.
- **LGBTQ rights, voting rights, civil rights** — supports holding the line ("Let's not go backwards"). Frames LGBTQ as a "Hall of Fame" movement.
- **Critique of incumbents** — uses extremely sharp personal language about Rep. Raskin (e.g., "Jurassic Raskin," accusations of "monopolizing local and national politics for over 60 years with his family relations," labeling this "nepotyranny"). Listeners should weigh this on its own terms; the rhetorical register is unlike most primary challengers in MD-8.

A note on tone, for the show's hosts: the campaign website contains passages — including a section on Montgomery County Public Schools — that use coined acronyms (e.g., MBN, REAJHG, LACAILUS PCG, AFAB, BEBCW, EIMC) to characterize racial, ethnic, and immigrant groups in ways that several mainstream civil-rights frameworks would identify as racially charged. We are flagging this neutrally so the on-air conversation can address it accurately rather than ignore it.

## Step 6 — Stance on listener-flagged topics

### Andrew Friedson's fiscal record
Kumar's website does not name Andrew Friedson by name. Kumar describes himself as a "pragmatic capitalist" who believes in a "hybrid Capitalism" with government rules where necessary. There is **no direct comparison to Friedson** on fiscal restraint vs. spending discipline from Kumar's platform.

### Wootton High School closure (MCPS)
**Kumar does not address the Wootton closure on his published platform.** His K-10 education proposal is a broader structural overhaul of MCPS, not a school-specific position. **Bottom line: unclear — no public statement found.**

### Sanctuary policy and undocumented immigration
This is the topic where Kumar is most clearly on the record. He calls for **employer-based enforcement with "very heavy penalties"** and an **expanded H-2B program**, and his framing of "Latin American and Central American" undocumented residents is harsh in tone. **Bottom line: he opposes sanctuary-style policies** and is, of the four Democratic primary candidates in MD-8, the most explicit opponent of sanctuary frameworks. (For comparison, incumbent Raskin's record is strongly pro-sanctuary, including his "No Secret Police Act" and votes against the Laken Riley Act; we will document Raskin's record in his own dossier on his series week.)

## Compiler's bottom line for the host team

J. D. Kumar is one of three primary challengers to Jamie Raskin in MD-8. His public footprint is dominated by his own campaign website, which is unusually long, idiosyncratically written, and contains policy frames — including on race, immigration, and the public schools — that need to be quoted accurately rather than paraphrased. **Episode 1 ("Origins")** has enough material to work with on the immigration-from-India / Queens / Montgomery County life arc, the UMD degrees, and the early-employment ethic, all sourced to the candidate's own statements. **Episode 2 ("Career")** is the thinnest — the telecoms claim is the spine and we don't have a firm name. **Episode 3 ("Political Record")** is structurally bare because he has never held office; the episode should pivot toward his stated policy program. **Episode 4 ("This Race")** is the meat — the contrast with Raskin, the MD-8 sanctuary/immigration question the listener has been asking about for weeks, and the open question of whether a candidate with this small a footprint can credibly make the case for replacing a five-term incumbent in a D+30 seat.

Where the host script needs a fact this dossier can't supply, **have a host say "we don't have a public record of X yet" rather than invent.** That is the rule for this candidate above all others on the schedule.
