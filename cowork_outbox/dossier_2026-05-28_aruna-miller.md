## Step 1 — Identity resolution

The Aruna Miller on the 2026 ballot is the sitting Lieutenant Governor of Maryland, sworn in January 2023 on Wes Moore's ticket. She is the same Aruna Miller who served three terms in the Maryland House of Delegates from District 15 (2010–2019), and who ran unsuccessfully in the 2018 Democratic primary for Maryland's 6th Congressional District [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aruna_Miller]. No identity ambiguity.

- **Age on primary day (June 23, 2026):** 61
- **Immigration:** Family came to the U.S. when she was seven. Raised in Poughkeepsie, New York, where her father Rao Katragadda worked as a mechanical engineer at IBM.
- **Current residence:** Upcounty Montgomery County (long-time Darnestown / D15 area through her Delegate years).
- **Family:** Married to David Miller; three daughters [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aruna_Miller].
- **Religion / ethnicity:** Telugu Hindu background; Indian American.
- **Languages:** English; family Telugu background.

- **Compensation:** Lieutenant Governor's statutory salary (FY26 in the ~$170K range per Maryland salary schedule).
- **Pre-elected career:** 25-year career as a transportation engineer with Montgomery County Department of Transportation; reached manager of transportation facility planning [src: https://governor.maryland.gov/leadership/lt-governor].
- **Disclosures:** Files annual Maryland State Ethics Commission disclosure as a statewide officeholder. No published ethics finding against her. No major business interests or stock holdings have generated regional press attention.

### C. Education
- **Undergraduate:** Bachelor of Science, Civil Engineering, Missouri University of Science and Technology (formerly University of Missouri-Rolla) [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aruna_Miller].
- No graduate degree publicly listed. The engineering credential anchored her career through 2010.

### D. Professional history (reverse chronological)

- **2019 – 2022:** Post-Delegate, post-congressional-loss period. ## Step 3 — Deep news history (lookback ~41 years to 1985)

### Engineering era (1985–2010)
Twenty-five years inside Montgomery County DOT made Miller a known quantity in county transportation planning before she ever ran for office. Her platform when she first ran for the Maryland House in 2010 leaned on that technical credential — I-270 corridor planning, MARC service to the upcounty, county transit equity, capital-budget allocation logic.

### Maryland House of Delegates, 2010–2019 (District 15)
Won the 2010 Democratic primary; held the seat across three terms (2011, 2015, 2019 sessions). D15 covers upcounty Montgomery County — Poolesville, Darnestown, Germantown corridor, immediately northwest of Rockville. Committee record:
- 2011–2015: Ways and Means (tax policy, education-funding formula).
- 2015–2019: Appropriations; vice chair of Transportation & Environment Subcommittee; vice chair of Capital Budget Subcommittee. Heavy involvement in MARC, Purple Line, and capital-budget allocations for Montgomery County [src: https://mgaleg.maryland.gov/mgawebsite/members/details/miller%20a?ys=2018rs].
- First Indian American woman elected to the Maryland Legislature [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aruna_Miller].

### 2018 congressional primary (MD-6)
After John Delaney announced he would not seek re-election in MD-6, Miller ran in the crowded 2018 Democratic primary. Finished second in a multi-candidate field to David Trone, who self-funded heavily. Did not return to the House — her Delegate term ended January 2019 — and used the 2019–2022 window for nonprofit board work and statewide profile-building.

### 2022 election to Lt. Gov.
Joined Wes Moore's ticket as his running mate. The Moore-Miller ticket won the July 2022 Democratic primary, then took the November 2022 general election decisively against Republican Dan Cox. Sworn in January 2023. Made history as the first South Asian woman elected lieutenant governor in the United States, the first Asian American lieutenant governor of Maryland, and the first immigrant to hold statewide office in Maryland [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aruna_Miller].

### Lt. Gov. record, 2023–present
Moore administration assigned her early-childhood education, child poverty, workforce development, and economic high-growth-industries portfolios [src: https://governor.maryland.gov/leadership/lt-governor]. Implementation lead on the ENOUGH Act (concentrated-child-poverty initiative). Frequent administration spokesperson on Montgomery County transit issues given her D15 background.

### 2026 re-election
Filed February 23, 2026, jointly with Moore. The Moore-Miller ticket is the incumbent Democratic ticket; primary opposition from within the party has been limited as of the May 28 snapshot. November 2026 will bring the real contest.

## Step 4 — Political track record

### Delegate-era legislation (representative; not exhaustive)
Transportation funding and capital-budget allocations dominated her sponsored work on Appropriations (2015–2019). On Ways and Means (2011–2015), she was active on tax and education-funding-formula questions. Bill-by-bill specifics are searchable on mgaleg.maryland.gov; this update has not pulled the full sponsored-bill list. Recommend pulling before any episode that walks through her vote record.

### Lt. Gov.-era policy themes
- **ENOUGH Act / child poverty:** Central Moore-Miller first-term push. Miller has been a public face of the implementation.
- **Workforce / high-growth industries:** Apprenticeship expansion; tech-sector workforce work.
- **Public education:** Early-childhood expansion; Blueprint for Maryland's Future implementation.
- **Transportation:** Less visible as a Lt. Gov. portfolio item than her D15 background might suggest; she defers operationally to the Secretary of Transportation.

### Endorsements
The full Maryland Democratic establishment on re-election, given incumbency. No serious primary opposition documented as of May 28.

## Step 5 — Controversies and notable press

No documented criminal record. No ethics finding against her. The 2018 congressional loss to Trone is the most consequential setback — treated as a strategic loss (Trone self-funded $13M+), not a scandal. The Moore administration as a whole has navigated FY26 and FY27 budget stress as federal funding compressed Maryland revenue; Miller has been a partner in administration coverage of that. No personally-attributed controversies surfaced in this update.

## Step 6 — Listener-flagged topics

- **Pre-political biographies of incumbents:** Miller's pre-political life is unusually well-documented (Hyderabad birth, IBM father in Poughkeepsie, civil engineering at Missouri S&T, 25 years at Montgomery County DOT). The listener can fact-check her.
- **Sanctuary / immigration framework:** Miller is herself an immigrant. She has consistently been on the pro-immigrant-protection side as both Delegate and Lt. Gov. The Moore-Miller administration has not moved to roll back Maryland-level immigrant protections.
- **MCPS funding / Wootton:** Lt. Gov. does not directly set MCPS appropriations (those are county-level). However, the Blueprint for Maryland's Future state-formula work — which she has been part of through the Lt. Gov. office — feeds the state share of MCPS funding. Her general posture is that the Blueprint is a floor, not a ceiling.
- **Ben Kramer alignment:** Kramer's Senate years overlap Miller's Delegate years; the two were generally aligned on consumer-protection and judiciary issues.
- **Friedson fiscal-restraint contrast:** Friedson is county; Miller is state. No direct contrast.
- **MoCo Board of Education candidates / judicial races / Register of Wills / Republican alternatives:** Not within Lt. Gov. portfolio. The listener has those decisions separately on the same ballot.
