# Marilyn Balcombe — Candidate Dossier

**Office:** Montgomery County Council, District 2 (Germantown, Clarksburg, Damascus, Poolesville, Boyds, parts of Gaithersburg)
**Party:** Democrat
**Listener-ballot status:** **Not on the listener's primary ballot.** The listener votes in Council District 7 (Luedtke). Balcombe represents District 2. Balcombe is included in this research file because she co-sponsors legislation with candidates the listener WILL vote on (notably Friedson on housing) and because she endorsed Friedson for County Executive on 2026-04-23.
**Dossier compiled:** 2026-05-19 by the politic_scan AI backend.

## 1. Resolved identity

Marilyn Balcombe, elected to the Montgomery County Council for the new District 2 seat in 2022 (the seat was created by the 2020 charter amendment expanding the council to 11 members with 7 districts). First-term councilmember. President and CEO of the Gaithersburg-Germantown Chamber of Commerce from 2008–2022 before her council run.

---

## 2. Biographical foundation

**Demographics.** Lives in Germantown with her family. Two adult children. Born and raised in upstate New York; moved to Maryland in the 1980s.

**Education.** B.S. from Hofstra University; M.B.A. from the University of Maryland Smith School of Business.

**Career.** Long career in upper Montgomery County economic development. CEO of the Gaithersburg-Germantown Chamber of Commerce 2008–2022. Prior roles at Montgomery College in workforce development. Active in the Maryland Innovation and Security Institute (cybersecurity workforce).

---

## 3. Council record (2023–present)

**Committees.** Economic Development (chair), Transportation and Environment.

**Signature legislation co-sponsored or led:**
- **More Housing N.O.W. package** (ZTA 25-02). Co-sponsor with Friedson and Fani-González. Passed 8–3 on 2025-07-22.
- **Bill 2-25** affordable housing measure. Co-sponsor with Friedson, Luedtke, Sayles, and Stewart.
- **Trust Act (Bill 32-25)**, sanctuary-style measure. Co-sponsor. Passed unanimously December 2025.
- **County Values Act (Bill 3-26)**, immigration enforcement signage and protections. Co-sponsor (lead: Mink). Passed unanimously 2026-03-24.
- **Bill 13-25** unlicensed commercial house parties. Voted yes.

**Budget votes.** Voted yes on FY25 operating budget. Voted yes on FY26 operating budget. Generally aligned with Friedson on tax-rate restraint while voting for the headline operating budgets.

**Endorsement.** Endorsed Andrew Friedson for County Executive on 2026-04-23, alongside District 7's Dawn Luedtke. ([Bethesda Magazine — Balcombe / Luedtke endorsement, 2026-04-23](https://bethesdamagazine.com/2026/04/23/balcombe-luedtke-endorse-friedson-county-executive/))
