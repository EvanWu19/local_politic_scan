## Step 1 — Resolving the person

There are several public-figure "Alec Stones" — including an actor and a political scientist (Alec Stone Sweet). The District 19 candidate is **Alec J. Stone**, MA, MPA, CNE, who has spent his career in healthcare-association management and federal policy advocacy in Washington, DC, and lives in Kemp Mill. His campaign treasurer is Elissa Weisblatt, and the campaign committee is **Friends of Alec Stone** [src: votealecstone.com]. The Bethesda Magazine 3/2/2026 District 19 roundup names him explicitly as "Alec Stone … a nonprofit manager from Kemp Mill" [src: bethesdamagazine.com/2026/03/02/five-new-candidates-two-incumbents-vie-for-three-district-19-delegate-seats/]. He is also recognizable by his LinkedIn handle "alec-stone-ma-mpa-05b93239" and his earlier X handle @ONSAlec from his Oncology Nursing Society years [src: linkedin.com/in/alec-stone-ma-mpa-05b93239; twitter.com/onsalec].

**Birth year — NOT publicly confirmed.** No source consulted (campaign site, AVAHO bio, LegiStorm summary, NACNS welcome letter, the Bethesda Magazine profile) gives a birth year or age. We can bracket it loosely: he has "more than two decades" of nonprofit-management experience [src: avaho.org/2024/02/26/meet-alec-stone-avahos-advocacy-consultant/], has four adult children and three grandchildren [src: votealecstone.com], and earned three degrees (BA + two master's) plus a CNE certificate. That sketch is consistent with an estimated age of mid-50s to mid-60s — call it **~55-65**, with **~60** as a reasonable central estimate. **Lookback window for step-3 news history therefore = max(40, 10) = 40 years**, i.e., approximately 1985 forward.

### B. Socioeconomic

- **Public financial disclosures:** As a non-incumbent candidate, no Maryland legislator personal financial disclosure exists. State campaign-finance reports through the Maryland State Board of Elections are pending (the 2026 pre-primary report is due May 26, 2026, and a more detailed financial disclosure must be filed within 30 days of swearing-in if he wins). No FEC disclosures (federal candidate filings) apply — this is a state-legislative race.
- **Income source:** Executive Director of two healthcare associations (see professional history below) and a current advocacy consultant for AVAHO [src: avaho.org/2024/02/26/meet-alec-stone-avahos-advocacy-consultant/]. Trade-association executive directors at organizations of NACNS's size typically draw six-figure compensation, but the specific NACNS and NATSAP Form 990 line items for Stone are not yet public for his tenure (his NACNS tenure began Aug/Sept 2025 and the corresponding 990 will not be filed until late 2026).
- **Home ownership:** not publicly disclosed (no MD SDAT search performed in this dossier round; an open question for next pass).
- **Business interests / stock holdings:** none publicly disclosed.
- **Disclosed conflicts of interest:** **Significant — flag for downstream episodes.** Stone is the sitting Executive Director of NATSAP (the National Association of Therapeutic Schools and Programs) and NACNS (the National Association of Clinical Nurse Specialists). Both are 501(c)(6) trade associations whose members are regulated, in part, by state-level legislation. NATSAP in particular represents residential therapeutic schools and wilderness-therapy programs, a sector that has been the subject of legislative oversight efforts in multiple states following high-profile tragedies (e.g., the Trails Carolina youth-death case in 2024) [src: members.natsap.org/natsappolicydigest/Details/natsap-statement-on-trails-carolina-218512]. If elected, the obvious recusal/conflict question is whether Maryland's youth-residential-treatment regulatory framework intersects with NATSAP's interests. Likewise, his decade-plus at the Oncology Nursing Society and current role at NACNS make him a healthcare-association insider whose disclosed corporate partnership history includes GlaxoSmithKline, Procter & Gamble, Janssen, Merck, Pfizer, and Colgate-Palmolive [src: avaho.org/2024/02/26/meet-alec-stone-avahos-advocacy-consultant/]. He would not be the first lobbyist-turned-legislator in Annapolis, but the listener should know going in that his professional life has been spent representing healthcare-trade interests on Capitol Hill.

## Step 3 — Deep news history (window: ~1985 forward, 40-year lookback against estimated age ~60)

Despite a 40-year nominal window, Stone's news footprint is concentrated in three eras: (a) his decade-plus at Oncology Nursing Society as a frequent named source on cancer-care advocacy, (b) his transition to NATSAP and AVAHO consultancy in 2023-24, and (c) his 2025 NACNS appointment plus his 2026 District 19 candidacy.

**a) Oncology Nursing Society era (2010-2023).** Stone is named in dozens of ONS Voice and oncology-trade publications discussing federal policy priorities (Medicare oncology-care coverage, drug-shortage legislation, oral parity legislation, palliative-care workforce bills). Two examples: (i) the December 2019 ONS Voice article noting that two ONS health-policy priorities passed the U.S. House thanks to that year's Capitol Hill Days [src: ons.org/publications-research/voice/advocacy/12-2019/two-ons-health-policy-priorities-pass-house-thanks]; (ii) the ONS podcast Episode 69 "Nurses Influence Health Policy at ONS's Capitol Hill Days" with Stone as guest [src: ons.org/education-hub/oncology-nursing-podcast/episode-69-nurses-influence-health-policy-onss-capitol-hill]. He published a 2014 article in Clinical Journal of Oncology Nursing titled "Voices of Oncology Nursing Society members matter in advocacy and decisions related to U.S. Health Policy" [src: pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25427709/].

**b) NATSAP and AVAHO years (2023-2024).** Stone took the NATSAP Executive Director role in September 2023 and was named AVAHO's advocacy consultant in a February 26, 2024 announcement [src: avaho.org/2024/02/26/meet-alec-stone-avahos-advocacy-consultant/]. His NATSAP tenure overlapped with the **Trails Carolina case** — a wilderness-therapy program member-organization that lost a 12-year-old client in February 2024, leading NATSAP to publish a position statement separating itself from the incident [src: members.natsap.org/natsappolicydigest/Details/natsap-statement-on-trails-carolina-218512]. How Stone managed NATSAP's public-affairs response to that case is a fair district-policy interview question.

**c) NACNS appointment and 2026 campaign (2025-2026).** Stone introduced himself to NACNS members in a 9/29/2025 letter that emphasized "science, medicine, and healthcare are under assault" — language echoing the second-Trump-administration political moment [src: nacns.org/2025/09/welcome-message-from-the-new-nacns-executive-director-alec-stone/]. He filed for District 19 delegate on **2/23/2026** (the day before MD's 2/24/2026 candidate-filing deadline) [src: bethesdamagazine.com/2026/03/02/five-new-candidates-two-incumbents-vie-for-three-district-19-delegate-seats/].

## Step 4 — Political track record

**Stone has not held elective office. He has no roll-call voting record.** That is a meaningful distinction from his three "incumbent-coordinated" District 19 competitors (Sen. Kramer, Del. Crutchfield, Del. Stewart). It means every claim about how he will vote — including the four priorities on his campaign site — is a campaign promise, not a record.

### Stated campaign priorities, verbatim from votealecstone.com:

- **Affordable Healthcare:** "Alec will work to make healthcare more affordable and accessible to all Marylanders. He has spent decades in the healthcare space and advocating for patients — he has the right experience to lower costs and improve the quality of care." [src: votealecstone.com]
- **Keeping Our Communities Safe:** "Alec will work to ensure that our communities are safe to raise a family and make sure our first responders have the resources they need to get the job done." [src: votealecstone.com]
- **Strengthen Our Economy:** "Alec will make it easier to start a business here in Maryland and support efforts to supercharge our economy and open new opportunities for everyone in our community. That means investing in our schools and attracting jobs with higher wages." [src: votealecstone.com]
- **Stand up to Trump:** "Alec will stand up to Trump's and ICE's authoritarian attacks on our communities and protect our democratic values." [src: votealecstone.com]

### Sponsored / co-sponsored legislation

None. Stone has never held the office.

### Public statements / policy positions with detail

- **On healthcare cost and access:** broadly framed but not specifying a policy mechanism on his campaign site. His career biography — 20+ years working for cancer-nursing, dental, medical, and pharmaceutical advocacy organizations — leaves him substantively conversant with insurance markets, Medicare reimbursement, drug pricing, and clinical-workforce supply, but he has not (publicly, by 2026-05-11) staked out a position on, e.g., a Maryland public option, MD's Prescription Drug Affordability Board reauthorization, or expansion of the Maryland Children's Health Program.

This is the pro-sanctuary side of the listener's recurring question. It is the strongest signal in the publicly available record — Stone is **opposed** to expanded ICE enforcement in Maryland communities and frames such enforcement as authoritarian.
- **On the Wootton High School closure / MCPS facilities decisions:** **No public statement.** The Wootton closure is a Montgomery County Board of Education decision under the MCPS Capital Improvements Program, not a General Assembly matter. As with the other 2026 District 19 candidates, Stone has not addressed it publicly.

Stone's prior board service on JCRC is a relevant lens for downstream votes on, e.g., MD school-curriculum bills or anti-BDS measures. Not a controversy — but a substantive lens.
