## Step 1 — Identity resolution

The Karla Silvestre on the 2026 At-Large Council ballot is the same Karla Silvestre who is an at-large member (and former president) of the Montgomery County Board of Education. She assumed the school-board seat in 2019 (elected November 2018) and her current BoE term ends December 1, 2026 [src: https://ballotpedia.org/Karla_Silvestre]. She is leaving the school board to run for Council rather than seeking BoE re-election [src: https://www.thebanner.com/politics-power/local-government/karla-silvestre-montgomery-county-council-SJFHHCBWE5C2HPL3FYTNH3EQPY/]. No identity ambiguity: campaign committee, school-board record, and Montgomery College employment all align to one person.

## Step 3 — Deep news history (lookback window: est. age ~52 → use full available record back to 2018 BoE entry; pre-2018 record is largely personal/professional, not electoral)

- **2018 (first run):** Ran for the At-Large Board of Education seat. In the June 26, 2018 nonpartisan primary she placed second (27.9%) behind Julie Reiley (32.0%) in an eight-candidate field. In the November 6, 2018 general she defeated Reiley 50.8% to 48.4% — a close, ~7,000-vote win out of ~291,000 cast [src: https://ballotpedia.org/Karla_Silvestre].
- **2022 (re-election):** Won the July 19, 2022 nonpartisan primary with 55.8% (first place). Won the November 8, 2022 general decisively over Mike Erickson, 69.9% to 29.3% [src: https://ballotpedia.org/Karla_Silvestre].
- **2023–2024:** Served as President of the Board of Education [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2025/09/16/silvestre-election-county-council/].
- **Sept 16, 2025:** Announced At-Large County Council campaign — ninth entrant; framed her BoE budget oversight (~half of the county's roughly $7B budget flows to MCPS) as direct preparation for the Council [src: https://www.mymcmedia.org/school-board-member-silvestre-announces-candidacy-for-county-council/] [src: https://bethesdamagazine.com/2025/09/16/silvestre-election-county-council/].

## Step 4 — Political track record (Board of Education)

- **Budget / fiscal role:** Chairs the BoE Fiscal Management Committee and twice served as board president; her central pitch is that she has overseen the largest single piece of the county budget (MCPS) and understands the funding fight from inside [src: https://www.thebanner.com/education/k-12-schools/school-board-montgomery-county-yang-silvestre-DYN6SB3FW5F7PFUEWE2EPB36XM/].
- **Housing (Council platform):** Supports rent stabilization but argues it is not sufficient alone — "Rent stabilization protects tenants from sudden, destabilizing rent spikes. But on its own, it is not solving our affordability crisis... it does not create housing." Backs "missing middle" supply: duplexes, triplexes, townhomes and small apartment buildings, especially near transit, plus pathways to homeownership [src: https://karlasilvestre.com/].
- **Transportation:** Champions "quick build" projects — safety and mobility improvements in weeks/months rather than years [src: https://karlasilvestre.com/].
